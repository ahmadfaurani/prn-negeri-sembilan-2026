## 1. Mission

This framework establishes the Priority Intelligence Requirements for the Nomination Day phase of the PRN Negeri Sembilan 2026 operation. It supplements the existing PRN16 framework (NS-01 through NS-18) with 25 Nomination Day-specific PIRs designed to produce real-time, actionable intelligence during the most operationally critical 24-hour window of the election cycle.

**Operating Principle:** During the Nomination Day window, collection cadence shifts from daily to hourly. Every intelligence product must support an immediate operational decision.

**Relationship to Existing Framework:**
- **NS-01 through NS-18** (PRN16 Standard Framework) — remain active; daily cadence continues
- **PIR-01 through PIR-25** (Nomination Day Surge) — activated 18 July 2026; hourly cadence; supersede standard reporting during nomination window

## 2. Nomination Day Priority Intelligence Requirements

### Candidate Configuration & Nomination Status

**PIR-01** — What is the final confirmed candidate configuration in every DUN?
- Essential Elements of Information: Candidate name, party, symbol, age, occupation, incumbent status and Returning Officer confirmation.

**PIR-02** — Which nomination papers are rejected, challenged or placed under legal review?
- EEI: DUN, candidate, rejection grounds, objection party, ruling and appeal intentions.

**PIR-03** — Which announced candidates fail to submit their nomination papers?
- EEI: No-shows, late arrivals, withdrawals, substitutions and reasons provided.

**PIR-04** — What is the final contest type for each constituency?
- EEI: Straight fight, three-cornered, four-cornered or wider contest; parties splitting similar voter blocs.

**PIR-05** — Which DUNs experience unexpected candidate substitutions or last-minute deployment changes?
- EEI: Original candidate, replacement, decision authority, timing and internal reaction.

### Coalition Dynamics & Operational Arrangements

**PIR-06** — What is the actual operational arrangement between BN, PN, PH and smaller parties?
- EEI: Seat non-competition, informal cooperation, reciprocal support, shared machinery and conflicting statements.

**PIR-07** — Which constituencies emerge as the highest-priority battlegrounds after nominations close?
- EEI: Previous majority, incumbent strength, candidate quality, demographic fit and multi-cornered vote fragmentation.

### Candidate Quality & Demographic Alignment

**PIR-08** — Which candidates possess the strongest local demographic and community alignment?
- EEI: Local origin, ethnicity, age, professional background, association membership, religious networks and service record.

**PIR-09** — Which candidates face immediate credibility or eligibility vulnerabilities?
- EEI: Criminal proceedings, bankruptcy, unresolved party discipline, qualification disputes, residency questions or misleading biography claims.

**PIR-10** — Where are independent candidates contesting, and whose support base are they most likely to divide?
- EEI: Political history, former party affiliation, sponsor network, community influence and expected vote source.

**PIR-11** — Which incumbent assembly members have been dropped, moved or replaced?
- EEI: Previous DUN, replacement candidate, stated justification, supporter reaction and sabotage risk.

**PIR-12** — Where are party grassroots openly rejecting the nominated candidate?
- EEI: Boycotts, protest gatherings, closed operations rooms, removal of flags, hostile statements and resignations.

### Ground Operations & Machinery Assessment

**PIR-13** — Which candidates demonstrate the strongest Nomination Day machinery?
- EEI: Supporter mobilisation, transport, volunteer discipline, branch participation, media coordination and command structure.

**PIR-14** — Which parties fail to mobilise credible ground support at nomination centres?
- EEI: Low attendance, weak branch representation, poor logistics, internal disorganisation and absent senior leaders.

**PIR-15** — What do Nomination Day crowd patterns reveal about comparative enthusiasm?
- EEI: Estimated attendance, supporter composition, youth participation, local versus imported supporters and crowd retention.

### Narrative, Sentiment & Information Operations

**PIR-16** — What are the first dominant campaign narratives introduced by each candidate and coalition?
- EEI: Economy, cost of living, identity, governance, local development, national leadership and candidate integrity.

**PIR-17** — Which candidate announcements generate the strongest positive or negative public reaction?
- EEI: Engagement velocity, sentiment, organic comments, local WhatsApp response and influencer amplification.

**PIR-18** — What misinformation, manipulated media or coordinated narratives emerge during nomination?
- EEI: False withdrawals, fake candidate lists, forged documents, edited videos, impersonation accounts and coordinated hashtags.

**PIR-19** — Which seeder and amplifier networks are activated for each political bloc?
- EEI: Originating accounts, amplification clusters, content synchronisation, reach, geography and cross-platform coordination.

### Security, Legal & Procedural

**PIR-20** — Are there security incidents capable of disrupting nominations or shaping public perception?
- EEI: Provocation, clashes, road obstruction, threats, arrests, prohibited materials and police intervention.

**PIR-21** — Which procedural or legal complaints are being prepared by political parties?
- EEI: Nomination objections, election-offence allegations, unauthorised campaigning, candidate eligibility and police reports.

**PIR-22** — Which senior political leaders personally attend each nomination centre, and what does their deployment indicate?
- EEI: Leader location, duration, endorsed candidate, media message and constituencies receiving limited leadership attention.

### Endorsements & Resource Assessment

**PIR-23** — Which candidates secure endorsements from influential local power structures?
- EEI: Community leaders, traditional institutions, religious figures, business groups, NGOs, youth organisations and former elected representatives.

**PIR-24** — Where are campaign-resource disparities immediately visible?
- EEI: Operations centres, vehicles, flags, digital advertising, volunteers, data capability, event production and funding indicators.

### Post-Nomination Watch

**PIR-25** — What immediate post-nomination events could materially change constituency assessments?
- EEI: Defections, resignations, candidate controversies, alliance disputes, viral incidents, legal action or machinery withdrawal.

## 3. Immediate Command-Centre Outputs

By T+2 hours after nominations close, the intelligence team must produce:

1. **Verified 36-DUN candidate roll** with confidence ratings
2. **Contest configuration map** identifying straight and multi-cornered contests
3. **Top 10 battleground constituency list**
4. **Candidate substitution, rejection and withdrawal alert report**
5. **Grassroots acceptance and sabotage-risk matrix**
6. **Nomination Day sentiment and narrative dashboard**
7. **Security, misinformation and legal-incident register**
8. **Initial candidate demographic-alignment assessment**

---

## 4. Critical Escalation Triggers

Immediately escalate any of the following to the State Intelligence Director via Telegram:

- Candidate rejection or unexpected withdrawal in a priority DUN
- Unannounced alliance, seat concession or reciprocal-support arrangement
- Grassroots boycott involving divisional or branch leadership
- Security incident involving candidates or senior political leaders
- Viral allegation capable of materially damaging candidate credibility
- Confirmed coordinated misinformation targeting election administration
- Independent candidate capable of splitting the decisive voter segment
- Candidate deployment that fundamentally changes the projected state majority

## 7. Confidence Ratings (Nomination Day Specific)

| Rating | Standard | Example |
|--------|----------|---------|
| **CONFIRMED** | SPR/RO official announcement or multiple wire services | "SPR confirms Razali Abu Samah (PN) for N13 Sikamat" |
| **HIGH** | 2+ mainstream news sources, consistent reporting | "NST and Sinar Harian report 25 BN candidates" |
| **MODERATE** | Single mainstream source or multiple social sources | "Astro Awani reports PKR candidate for N14" |
| **LOW** | Single social source or unverified WhatsApp | [UNVERIFIED] "WhatsApp claim: X dropped for N06" |
| **DISPUTED** | Conflicting reports from different sources | "BN claims 25 seats, PN claims 11 — overlap disputed" |

## 8. Integration with Existing Framework

The Nomination Day PIRs (PIR-01 through PIR-25) map to existing PRN16 PIRs as follows:

| Nomination Day PIR | Maps to PRN16 PIR | Enhancement |
|--------------------|--------------------|-------------|
| PIR-01 (Candidate config) | NS-10 (Candidate acceptance) | Hourly cadence, 36-DUN granularity |
| PIR-06 (Coalition arrangements) | NS-02 (Realignment), NS-11 (Opposition) | Operational detail, machinery sharing |
| PIR-07 (Battlegrounds) | NS-04 (DUN risk) | Post-nomination re-assessment |
| PIR-16 (Narratives) | NS-14 (Dominant narratives) | First-day campaign narratives |
| PIR-18 (Misinformation) | NS-15 (Misinformation risk) | Nomination-specific disinformation |
| PIR-25 (Post-nomination events) | NS-02 (Realignment) | Immediate watch items |

Standard PRN16 PIRs remain active throughout the nomination window at their normal cadence.
