# PRIORITY INTELLIGENCE REQUIREMENTS
## Monitoring of Non-Core Party Candidates — Negeri Sembilan State Election

**TLP:AMBER — Not for public release**

**Last updated:** 2026-07-16

---

## Monitoring Scope

This PIR framework covers candidates from non-core parties contesting the Negeri Sembilan State Election:
- **PAS** — particularly incumbent candidates in Paroi and Bagan Pinang
- **WAWASAN** — community-figure-based candidates
- **GERAKAN** — urban and non-Malay voter appeal
- **MIPP** — Indian electorate candidates
- **Independent/other minor party candidates** — including Danni Rais and similar figures

### Priority Constituencies for Non-Core Monitoring
N.04 Klawang, N.05 Serting, N.11 Lobak, N.13 Sikamat, N.14 Ampangan, N.21 Bukit Kepayang, N.23 Mambau, N.25 Paroi, N.30 Lukut, N.31 Bagan Pinang, N.34 Gemas

---

## PIR 1 — Candidate Confirmation and Political Status

**Primary question:** To what extent have these candidates been formally confirmed and endorsed by their respective parties?

**Information requirements:**
- Official confirmation of candidate name, party, and constituency.
- Candidate status: announced, proposed, shortlisted, or under negotiation.
- Current position within the party or affiliated organisations.
- Incumbent, former candidate, or first-time candidate status.
- Relationship with central and state party leadership.
- Risk of candidate replacement before nomination day.

**Warning indicators:**
- Official party denial.
- Overlapping seat claims.
- Grassroots objections.
- Change of party platform, logo, or nomination arrangement.

---

## PIR 2 — Strategic Rationale for Seat Selection

**Primary question:** Why has each party selected the respective candidate for that constituency?

**Information requirements:**
- Demographic, locality, and traditional support factors.
- Historical election results.
- Previous winning or losing margins.
- Candidate strength relative to party brand strength.
- Potential to split a specific voter bloc.
- Whether the seat is considered winnable, experimental, symbolic, or part of a negotiation strategy.

**Specific assessment focus:**
- PAS: potential expansion among Malay and conservative voters.
- WAWASAN: ability to build local influence through recognisable community figures.
- GERAKAN: potential appeal among urban and non-Malay voters.
- MIPP: ability to attract a portion of the Indian electorate.

---

## PIR 3 — Candidate Profile, Credibility, and Local Acceptance

**Primary question:** What is the actual level of candidate acceptance among voters and local communities?

**Information requirements:**
- Educational, professional, and community-service background.
- Length and depth of involvement in the constituency.
- Track record and key achievements.
- Relationships with resident associations, NGOs, mosques, temples, churches, business groups, and community organisations.
- Grassroots perception of integrity, competence, and accessibility.
- Candidate name recognition.
- Support from local branch leaders and party machinery.

**Indicators:**
- Programme attendance.
- Engagement on local social-media channels.
- Public endorsement by community leaders.
- Reception during ground visits and walkabouts.

---

## PIR 4 — Machinery Strength and Mobilisation Capability

**Primary question:** Does the party have sufficient machinery to convert political support into actual votes?

**Information requirements:**
- Number and distribution of operations centres and campaign rooms.
- Leadership structure at constituency, polling-district, and locality level.
- Number of active campaign workers.
- Polling and counting agent readiness.
- Voter transport capability.
- Voter-data and volunteer-management systems.
- Financial resources, campaign materials, and logistics.
- Cooperation with allied parties or external organisations.

**Warning indicators:**
- Shortage of campaign workers.
- Internal conflict.
- Machinery overly dependent on the candidate.
- Weak coverage of critical polling districts.

---

## PIR 5 — Candidate Strengths, Weaknesses, and Vulnerabilities

**Primary question:** What factors could materially strengthen or weaken each candidate's prospects?

**Information requirements:**
- Reported integrity, performance, or controversy issues.
- Conflict with party leadership or local communities.
- Misalignment between candidate profile and constituency demographics.
- Perception of being an outsider.
- Inconsistent, insensitive, or controversial public statements.
- Lawfully reported issues involving family, business, or political relationships.
- Weaknesses in communication, organisation, or mobilisation.

**Assessment classification:**
- Critical strength.
- Recoverable weakness.
- Serious vulnerability.
- Immediate reputational risk.

---

## PIR 6 — Electoral Impact and Vote-Splitting Potential

**Primary question:** Which candidate or party is most capable of changing the result even without winning?

**Information requirements:**
- Voter blocs most likely to shift.
- Major party most likely to be affected.
- Estimated vote splitting by ethnicity, age, locality, and political preference.
- Risk created by three-cornered or multi-cornered contests.
- Potential impact on the winning margin.
- Probability of deposit loss.
- Potential role as kingmaker or vote splitter.

**Priority constituencies:**
- N.04 Klawang
- N.05 Serting
- N.11 Lobak
- N.13 Sikamat
- N.14 Ampangan
- N.21 Bukit Kepayang
- N.23 Mambau
- N.25 Paroi
- N.30 Lukut
- N.31 Bagan Pinang
- N.34 Gemas

## PIR 7 — Campaign Narrative, Messaging, and Positioning

**Primary question:** What core narratives are being used to build voter support?

**Information requirements:**
- Recurring themes in speeches, campaign events, and social-media content.
- Local issues receiving the greatest emphasis.
- Positioning toward the state and federal governments.
- Economic, cost-of-living, religious, ethnic, development, and integrity narratives.
- Attacks against opposing candidates.
- Message variation across target voter groups.
- Candidate ability to contain and respond to negative issues.

**Monitoring channels:**
- Facebook.
- TikTok.
- Instagram.
- Open WhatsApp and Telegram channels.
- Campaign speeches and media statements.
- News portals and local media.

---

## PIR 8 — Leadership Support, Networks, and Political Sponsorship

**Primary question:** Who is providing political, organisational, or financial support to each candidate?

**Information requirements:**
- Participation by central and state party leaders.
- Support from former elected representatives or influential local figures.
- Links with NGOs, business associations, and community networks.
- Publicly reported sources of campaign support.
- Family ties or political patronage relationships.
- Possibility of informal cooperation between parties.

**Specific focus:**
- Danni Rais' relationship with Tan Sri Rais Yatim and its political impact.
- Incumbency advantage of PAS candidates in Paroi and Bagan Pinang.
- Community networks supporting the GERAKAN and MIPP candidates.

---

## PIR 9 — Voter Sentiment and Movement of Support

**Primary question:** How is voter sentiment toward each candidate changing throughout the campaign period?

**Information requirements:**
- Positive, neutral, and negative sentiment.
- Issues driving changes in support.
- Public reaction to candidate announcements.
- Differences between urban, semi-urban, and rural areas.
- Sentiment by age group and community.
- Level of soft support versus committed support.
- Issues capable of causing a sudden shift in voter behaviour.

**Reporting frequency:**
- Weekly before nomination.
- Every 72 hours after nomination.
- Daily during the final week of campaigning.

---

## PIR 10 — Electoral Outcome Assessment

**Primary question:** What is the likely performance of each candidate based on the latest available evidence?

**Required output for each candidate:**
- Probability of winning.
- Estimated vote range.
- Expected placement in the contest.
- Primary support blocs.
- Key victory factors.
- Key defeat factors.
- Analytical confidence level.
- Best-case, baseline, and worst-case scenarios.

**Assessment scale:**
- HIGH: Capable of winning or mounting a serious challenge.
- MODERATE: Capable of influencing the result or winning margin.
- LOW: Limited influence but may split votes.
- MINIMAL: Primarily symbolic campaign or high risk of losing deposit.

---

## OPERATIONAL REPORTING FORMAT

Each report should contain:
1. Key developments.
2. Newly verified information.
3. Changes in voter sentiment.
4. Assessed impact on the contest.
5. Risks and early-warning indicators.
6. Intelligence gaps.
7. Recommended next collection actions.
8. Confidence level: High, Moderate, or Low.

## ANALYTICAL RULES
- All information must be collected through lawful and ethical open-source methods.
- Facts, allegations, and analytical judgments must be clearly separated.
- Unverified information must be labelled accordingly and must not be presented as established fact.
