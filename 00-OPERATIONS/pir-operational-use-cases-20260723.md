### USE CASE-01: STR/SARA GROUND VERIFICATION

**PIR:** NS-07 (Voter Sentiment), NS-14 (Dominant Narratives)
**Decision Owner:** Insights Lead
**Trigger:** STR cash aid reduction narrative (RM100→RM50) corroborated across 3+ DUNs. PIR NS-07 escalation trigger met: "Sustained negative movement across three or more constituencies." Anwar announced SARA increase on 23 Jul at Paroi — but no ground-level verification of whether this has neutralised the narrative.

**Evidence Base:** CORROBORATED across N.02 Pertang, N.14 Ampangan, N.34 Gemas by multiple auditors. Confidence: HIGH. Direct quotes from respondents confirm the narrative is active and attributed to Anwar's government.

**Timeline Constraint:** Complete by 25 Jul (T-7) — 3 days from today. Must complete before 27 Jul to inform UC-03 deployment and UC-11 BN manifesto counter-messaging.

**Operational Objective:** Verify whether the 23 Jul SARA announcement has neutralised the STR reduction narrative at ground level. Determine whether additional counter-messaging is required. If the narrative persists, generate evidence base for UC-03 (FELDA Credit Reattribution Campaign).

---

**Phase 1: COLLECTION (24 hours — 23–24 Jul)**

1. Deploy 3 audit teams to N.02 Pertang, N.14 Ampangan, N.34 Gemas — the 3 DUNs where STR reduction was most acutely reported.
2. Re-visit respondents from the original audit: N.02 Pertang (norlaijan bt ibrahim, mad shah bin mad isa), N.14 Ampangan (zulkefli b sarif), N.34 Gemas (NORZELILAH BINTI MOHAMED).
3. Collect: (a) awareness of SARA increase announcement, (b) whether the announcement changes their view of PH government, (c) whether the STR reduction narrative is still being deployed by BN ketua kampung or social media, (d) whether respondents know SARA/STR originates from the PH federal government or from UMNO.
4. Who: Electoral Intelligence Cell auditors (3 teams, 2 per team).
5. Where: FELDA settlements and semi-urban localities in the 3 target DUNs.

**Phase 2: ANALYSIS (12 hours — 24–25 Jul)**

1. Cross-tabulate: SARA awareness vs narrative persistence. Decision criteria:
   - If 70%+ of respondents are aware of SARA increase AND report the STR narrative is weakening → narrative neutralised; UC-03 messaging focus shifts to credit attribution only.
   - If 50–70% awareness but narrative persists → partial neutralisation; UC-03 must address both STR reduction and credit attribution.
   - If <50% awareness OR narrative fully persists → narrative NOT neutralised; escalate to Insights Lead for emergency messaging deployment.
2. Assess whether the SARA announcement has been counter-framed by BN (Hamzah already framed it as "desperate election-time move" on 23 Jul).
3. Produce 1-page verification brief for Insights Lead with go/no-go recommendation for UC-03.

**Phase 3: OPERATIONAL DEPLOYMENT (24 hours — 25 Jul, if narrative persists)**

1. If SARA announcement has NOT neutralised the narrative: deploy targeted SARA/STR messaging through machinery channels — Malay-language talking points for BGU workers explaining: (a) STR was not reduced, it was restructured; (b) SARA increase is a PH government initiative, not UMNO; (c) the increase takes effect immediately.
2. Who: Narrative Intelligence Lead coordinates with Insights Lead; BGU teams in target DUNs deliver messaging.
3. Where: N.02 Pertang, N.14 Ampangan, N.34 Gemas — FELDA settlements, warga emas households, semi-urban localities.
4. Deploy house-to-house visits targeting warga emas (elderly) households — the audit identified elderly voters as high-turnout demographics with welfare information gaps.

**Phase 4: VERIFICATION & FEEDBACK (25–27 Jul)**

1. Re-deploy auditors to the same 3 DUNs for a 24-hour post-deployment check.
2. Measure: % of respondents who can correctly identify SARA/STR as a PH government initiative.
3. Feedback loop: Results feed into UC-03 (FELDA Credit Reattribution Campaign) messaging design and UC-11 (BN Manifesto Counter-Messaging) on jobs vs welfare framing.
4. Report to Insights Lead and State Intelligence Director daily until polling day.

**Decision Point:** Insights Lead decides by 25 Jul 18:00 whether the SARA narrative is neutralised (stand down) or requires emergency counter-messaging (activate Phase 3 and feed UC-03).

**Success Criteria:**
- 70%+ of re-audited respondents in target DUNs are aware of SARA increase by 27 Jul.
- 60%+ can correctly identify SARA/STR as a PH government (not UMNO) initiative by 27 Jul.
- STR reduction narrative drops from "top 3 voter concerns" in at least 2 of 3 target DUNs by 28 Jul.

**Escalation Path:** If success criteria not met by 27 Jul → escalate to State Intelligence Director → emergency statewide SARA messaging campaign deployed through all available channels (ceramah, social media, BGU networks) by 28 Jul. Escalation flag: ESC-STR-01.

**Resource Requirements:**
- Personnel: 6 auditors (3 teams of 2) for Phase 1; 3 auditors for Phase 4.
- Materials: SARA/STR Malay-language fact sheets (print), talking points for BGU.
- Funding: Transport and per diem for 6 auditors × 2 days; printing of fact sheets.

### USE CASE-02: GRASSROOTS RACIAL NARRATIVE COUNTER-DEPLOYMENT

**PIR:** NS-07 (Voter Sentiment), NS-11 (Opposition Strategy), NS-14 (Dominant Narratives)
**Decision Owner:** Narrative Intelligence Lead
**Trigger:** Racial narrative weaponization corroborated across 5+ DUNs. PIR NS-14 escalation trigger met: "A misleading narrative achieves rapid statewide reach." Narratives: "DAP kawal PMX," "sentimen perkauman," "melayu islam memerintah." Deployed through BN ketua kampung at "peringkat akar umbi."

**Evidence Base:** CORROBORATED across N.02 Pertang, N.03 Sungai Lui, N.13 Sikamat, N.10 Nilai, N.28 Kota by 5 different auditors. Confidence: HIGH. Critical finding: the narrative CAN be countered — "bila terangkan dia mcm faham yang semua itu hanya dakyah untuk menakutkan org melayu" (N.02 Pertang respondent). This is a persuasion opportunity, not a fixed position.

**Timeline Constraint:** Deploy by 27 Jul (T-5). Full deployment must complete before 28 Jul early voting. Countered messaging sustained through 31 Jul.

**Operational Objective:** Deploy a statewide, sustained, Malay-language counter-messaging strategy through alternative community channels that bypass BN-controlled ketua kampung. Neutralise the "DAP kontrol gov" and "Malay unity" narratives at grassroots level using direct, factual explanations that the audit has proven can change minds.

---

**Phase 1: COLLECTION (24 hours — 23–24 Jul)**

1. Map all localities in the 5+ DUNs where racial narrative is corroborated: Pertang, Sungai Lui, Sikamat, Nilai, Kota. Add Ampangan, Johol (NS-14 evidence).
2. Identify alternative community channels that bypass BN ketua kampung: mosque committees, surau leaders, NGO networks, youth associations, women's groups, market traders associations, retired teachers network.
3. Collect the specific racial narrative variants being deployed in each DUN — tailor counter-messaging to local variant.
4. Who: Narrative Intelligence Lead collection team + Opposition Analysis Cell.
5. Where: All 7 affected DUNs, with priority on N.10 Nilai (Vulnerable classification) and N.28 Kota (Competitive, polarised).

**Phase 2: ANALYSIS (12 hours — 24 Jul)**

1. Classify narrative variants by DUN:
   - Variant A: "DAP kawal PMX / DAP kontrol gov" — target: Malay civil servants, FELDA communities
   - Variant B: "Melayu Islam memerintah / nak melayu bersatu" — target: Malay women, elderly
   - Variant C: "Cina Tua dia basuh otak untuk sokong MCA BN" — target: Chinese elderly (requires Chinese-language counter-narrative)
2. Decision criteria:
   - If narrative is deployed through ketua kampung ONLY → counter through alternative channels (surau, NGO).
   - If narrative is deployed through social media AND ketua kampung → dual-track counter (physical + digital).
   - If narrative is deployed through ceramah/BN events → monitor and counter at PH events.
3. Identify the 3 most effective counter-narratives from the audit evidence:
   - Counter A: "DAP is one party in a coalition; PH is the coalition; Malays hold MB, MB posts"
   - Counter B: "Aminuddin owns 'boneka DAP' — 'boneka DAP yang tolong Melayu' via freehold land policy"
   - Counter C: "Racial narrative is a dakyah to scare Malay voters — the real issue is who delivers aid"

**Phase 3: OPERATIONAL DEPLOYMENT (72 hours — 25–27 Jul)**

1. Deploy 3 counter-narrative tracks simultaneously:
   - **Track A (Malay ground):** BGU workers equipped with Malay-language talking points (1-page fact sheet) delivering direct explanation at surau, market, community gatherings. Deploy to all 7 affected DUNs.
   - **Track B (Statewide ceramah):** Deploy Aminuddin's "boneka DAP yang tolong Melayu" counter-narrative beyond Sikamat to Ampangan, Pertang, Nilai, Johol. Schedule candidate appearances at all affected DUNs by 27 Jul.
   - **Track C (Digital):** Deploy short-form Malay-language video content (60-second clips) countering racial narrative on TikTok, WhatsApp, Facebook. Target: 18-40 Malay demographic in affected DUNs.
2. Who: Narrative Intelligence Lead coordinates; BGU teams deliver Track A; candidate scheduling via Candidate Management Lead; digital team delivers Track C.
3. Where: 7 DUNs — Pertang, Sungai Lui, Sikamat, Nilai, Kota, Ampangan, Johol.

**Phase 4: VERIFICATION & FEEDBACK (27–31 Jul)**

1. Deploy auditors to affected DUNs every 48 hours to measure narrative penetration.
2. Measure: % of respondents who have heard the counter-narrative; % who report changed perception of "DAP kontrol gov"; sentiment shift in narrative velocity tracking.
3. Feedback loop: Results feed daily to Narrative Intelligence Lead. If narrative is NOT weakening in a specific DUN after 48 hours, re-target Track A and Track B to that DUN.
4. Report to State Intelligence Director in daily brief.

**Decision Point:** Narrative Intelligence Lead decides by 25 Jul 12:00 whether to proceed with all 3 tracks or concentrate on 1–2 based on Phase 1/2 results. Reassess by 27 Jul whether Track C (digital) is reaching target demographic.

**Success Criteria:**
- Counter-narrative reaches 50%+ of sampled respondents in at least 5 of 7 target DUNs by 29 Jul.
- "DAP kontrol gov" narrative drops from "High" to "Moderate" volume in narrative velocity tracking by 30 Jul.
- At least 3 DUNs show measurable sentiment improvement (audit score increase or status stabilisation) by 30 Jul.
- N.10 Nilai (Vulnerable) stabilises — no further PH→BN shift recorded in post-deployment audit.

**Escalation Path:** If counter-narrative does not reach 30%+ penetration in any target DUN by 27 Jul → escalate to State Intelligence Director → deploy senior leadership (Aminuddin, Mat Sabu, Faiz Fadzil) for targeted ceramah in failing DUNs by 28 Jul. Escalation flag: ESC-NAR-02.

**Resource Requirements:**
- Personnel: 3 BGU teams (6 workers) per DUN × 7 DUNs = 42 ground workers; digital content team (3); candidate scheduling coordination (Candidate Management Lead).
- Materials: 1-page Malay-language fact sheets (5,000 copies), 60-second video clips (3 variants), WhatsApp broadcast templates.
- Funding: Printing (5,000 fact sheets); digital content production; transport for 42 ground workers × 3 days.

### USE CASE-03: FELDA CREDIT REATTRIBUTION CAMPAIGN

**PIR:** NS-05 (Locality Prioritisation), NS-07 (Voter Sentiment), NS-11 (Opposition Strategy), NS-14 (Dominant Narratives)
**Decision Owner:** Field Operations Lead
**Trigger:** BN-aligned ketua kampung claiming credit for PH government aid. Corroborated quote: "Yg bagi bantuan PH, yg dpt nama BN." This is Cross-PIR Theme 1 — the single most important strategic finding from the audit. PH is governing but not campaigning — government achievements are invisible at point of delivery because intermediaries are BN-aligned.

**Evidence Base:** CORROBORATED across N.34 Gemas, N.36 Repah, N.03 Sungai Lui by 2 different auditors. Confidence: HIGH. Pattern is structural, not campaign-period — BN-aligned ketua kampung act as permanent intermediaries.

**Timeline Constraint:** Complete deployment by 30 Jul (T-2). Initial deployment by 27 Jul to align with UC-02 racial narrative counter. Must complete after UC-01 (SARA verification) and UC-08 (ketua kampung mapping) deliver results.

**Operational Objective:** Close the credit attribution gap in FELDA areas. Ensure voters know that STR, SARA, Budi95, and other welfare aid originates from the PH federal government, not UMNO. Bypass BN-aligned ketua kampung by establishing direct PH community liaison channels in FELDA settlements.

---

**Phase 1: COLLECTION (48 hours — 24–25 Jul, feeds from UC-08)**

1. Receive BN Ketua Kampung Network Map from UC-08 — classification of all ketua kampung in FELDA DUNs as BN-aligned, PH-aligned, or neutral.
2. In BN-aligned FELDA settlements, identify alternative community access points: FELDA settlers' associations, cooperative committees, women's groups (FELCITA), youth committees, retired FELDA staff networks.
3. Collect specific aid delivery records: what PH government aid (STR, SARA, Budi95, flood relief) has been delivered to each FELDA settlement in the past 12 months. Cross-reference with government delivery data.
4. Who: Field Operations Lead deployment team + Opposition Analysis Cell (for ketua kampung map from UC-08).
5. Where: FELDA settlements in N.02 Pertang, N.03 Sungai Lui, N.06 Palong, N.34 Gemas, N.36 Repah.

**Phase 2: ANALYSIS (12 hours — 25 Jul)**

1. Map the credit attribution gap: for each FELDA settlement, identify (a) what aid was delivered by PH government, (b) whether ketua kampung claimed credit, (c) whether voters attribute the aid to UMNO or PH.
2. Decision criteria:
   - If >60% of FELDA voters attribute aid to UMNO → full reattribution campaign required (Phase 3 Track A+B+C).
   - If 40–60% attribute to UMNO → targeted reattribution in highest-impact settlements only (Track A+B).
   - If <40% attribute to UMNO → minimal intervention; monitor only.
3. Identify the 3 highest-impact FELDA settlements for initial deployment — where the gap is widest and the voter base is largest.
4. Design reattribution messaging: "Bantuan dari kerajaan PH, bukan dari UMNO" — factual, simple, Malay-language. Include specific aid amounts and delivery dates.

**Phase 3: OPERATIONAL DEPLOYMENT (72 hours — 26–29 Jul)**

1. **Track A (Direct community contact):** Deploy PH liaison teams to FELDA settlements, bypassing BN ketua kampung. Hold community sessions at FELDA settlers' association halls, surau, cooperative offices. Deliver factual reattribution messaging with specific aid data.
2. **Track B (Visual attribution):** Deploy campaign materials with explicit PH government branding: "SARA RM200 — Inisiatif Kerajaan Perpaduan" banners at FELDA entrances, community centres, surau. Ensure PH logo and "Kerajaan Perpaduan" are visible at aid delivery points.
3. **Track C (Witness testimony):** Recruit FELDA settlers who have received PH aid to testify at ceramah and in video content: "Saya dapat SARA dari kerajaan PH, bukan UMNO." Deploy on WhatsApp and TikTok.
4. Who: Field Operations Lead coordinates; BGU teams execute Track A; campaign material team executes Track B; digital team executes Track C.
5. Where: 5 FELDA DUNs — Pertang, Sungai Lui, Palong, Gemas, Repah. Priority: the 3 highest-impact settlements identified in Phase 2.

**Phase 4: VERIFICATION & FEEDBACK (29–31 Jul)**

1. Deploy auditors to FELDA settlements 48 hours after Track A deployment.
2. Measure: % of respondents who correctly attribute SARA/STR/Budi95 to PH government (not UMNO); % who have seen PH-branded campaign materials; % who report hearing reattribution messaging from non-BN channels.
3. Feedback loop: Results feed to Field Operations Lead and State Operations Director. If reattribution is not penetrating in a specific settlement, deploy Track A team for a second visit.
4. Report daily to State Intelligence Director.

**Decision Point:** Field Operations Lead decides by 25 Jul 18:00 which FELDA settlements receive full deployment (Track A+B+C) vs targeted deployment (Track A+B) based on Phase 2 gap analysis.

**Success Criteria:**
- 50%+ of FELDA respondents in target settlements can correctly identify PH government as the source of SARA/STR by 30 Jul.
- PH-branded campaign materials visible at all 5 target FELDA DUN settlements by 27 Jul.
- At least 3 FELDA settler witness testimonies deployed on WhatsApp/TikTok by 29 Jul.
- Credit attribution gap narrows: BN-aligned ketua kampung credit-claiming reported by fewer respondents in post-deployment audit.

**Escalation Path:** If reattribution messaging does not reach 30%+ of FELDA respondents in target settlements by 29 Jul → escalate to State Operations Director → deploy emergency ceramah with senior PH leadership (Aminuddin or federal minister) at failing settlements by 30 Jul. Escalation flag: ESC-CRD-03.

**Resource Requirements:**
- Personnel: 5 PH liaison teams (3 per team) for Track A; campaign material deployment team (5) for Track B; digital team (3) for Track C.
- Materials: PH-branded banners (20+), fact sheets with aid delivery data (3,000 copies), witness testimony video production.
- Funding: Banner production and placement; transport for 15 liaison team members × 4 days; ceramah logistics for escalation.

### USE CASE-04: PKR CANDIDATE-MACHINERY RECONCILIATION

**PIR:** NS-10 (Candidate Acceptance), NS-16 (Machinery Readiness)
**Decision Owner:** Candidate Management Lead
**Trigger:** PKR machinery friction corroborated in 2 DUNs. PIR NS-10 escalation trigger met: "Local machinery refuses to support candidate in competitive seats." **Evidence Base:** CORROBORATED in N.03 Sungai Lui (auditor: amirulredz19, respondent: Zamzul, PKR candidate coordination lead) and N.14 Ampangan (auditor: ryqall, respondent: Mohammed dainel hakimi). Confidence: MODERATE-HIGH. Both cases involve PKR and machinery-candidate friction.

**Timeline Constraint:** Resolve by 25 Jul (T-7). Must complete before 28 Jul early voting — machinery must be unified for GOTV operations.

**Operational Objective:** Resolve PKR candidate-machinery friction in N.03 Sungai Lui and N.14 Ampangan. Ensure local machinery is fully supporting the candidate for GOTV operations. Investigate the BN funding allegation in Sungai Lui. Resolve the cabang jurisdictional overlap in Ampangan.

---

1. **N.03 Sungai Lui:** Collect detailed information on the BN funding allegation against the PKR candidate. Interview Zamzul (candidate coordination lead) for specifics: who made the allegation, what is the evidence, who is spreading it within the machinery. Assess the candidate's local acceptance among non-machinery voters (the candidate is a former schoolteacher — leverage this).
2. **N.14 Ampangan:** Map the cabang structure — identify the "2 dalam 1 tempat 1 cabang 1 calon" overlap. Identify the ketua cabang in conflict with the candidate. Interview party members who booed the candidate at the announcement.

4. Who: Candidate Management Lead investigation team.
5. Where: N.03 Sungai Lui and N.14 Ampangan — direct field visits to PKR cabang offices.

**Phase 2: ANALYSIS (12 hours — 24 Jul)**

2. **N.14 Ampangan:**
   - Deploy PKR state leadership to resolve cabang overlap: appoint acting ketua cabang or redraw jurisdiction.
   - Hold a unity event: candidate + ketua cabang + machinery members publicly reconcile.
   - Leverage Anwar's endorsement: Anwar named Ampangan at Sikamat dinner — deploy this endorsement in Ampangan messaging.
3. Who: Candidate Management Lead deploys; PKR state leadership executes mediation.
4. Where: N.03 Sungai Lui and N.14 Ampangan — direct cabang-level intervention.

**Phase 4: VERIFICATION & FEEDBACK (26–28 Jul)**

1. Deploy auditors to both DUNs 48 hours after intervention to assess:
   - Machinery members report supporting the candidate (yes/no).
   - GOTV operations are functioning (polling agents assigned, voter lists being worked).
   - The BN funding allegation (Sungai Lui) has been neutralised or is still spreading.
   - The cabang overlap (Ampangan) has been resolved or persists.
2. Feedback loop: Results feed to Candidate Management Lead and State Operations Director. If friction persists, escalate.
3. Report to State Intelligence Director in daily brief.

**Decision Point:** Candidate Management Lead decides by 25 Jul 18:00 whether the candidate-machinery relationship is sufficiently reconciled for GOTV operations, or whether PKR state leadership must replace the candidate (Sungai Lui) or restructure cabang authority (Ampangan).

**Success Criteria:**
- Both DUNs upgrade from Amber to Green machinery readiness by 27 Jul.
- PKR machinery in both DUNs confirms candidate support and assigns polling agents by 27 Jul.
- BN funding allegation in Sungai Lui is investigated and resolved (cleared or escalated) by 25 Jul.
- Cabang overlap in Ampangan is structurally resolved with clear jurisdiction by 26 Jul.
- No further "booing" or public machinery dissent reported by 28 Jul.

**Escalation Path:** If machinery does not confirm candidate support by 26 Jul → escalate to PKR state chair → candidate replacement considered for Sungai Lui; PKR state chair directly manages Ampangan cabang until polling day. Escalation flag: ESC-CND-04.

**Resource Requirements:**
- Personnel: Candidate Management Lead investigation team (2); PKR state leadership (2 senior leaders); 1 dedicated machinery coordinator per DUN.
- Materials: "Bekas Cikgu" candidate profile materials (Sungai Lui); Anwar endorsement messaging (Ampangan); unity event logistics.
- Funding: Mediation meeting logistics; candidate profile printing; unity event costs.

**Evidence Base:** CORROBORATED across N.09 Lenggeng (auditor: hirmanarsha), N.34 Gemas (auditor: Ducuva), N.36 Repah (auditor: Ducuva). Confidence: HIGH. Machinery workers making "perbandingan dengan jentera BN" — morale is affected by visible resource disparity.

**Timeline Constraint:** Deploy materials by 26 Jul (T-6). Show of force must be visible before 28 Jul early voting. All materials in place before 31 Jul campaign end.

**Operational Objective:** Deploy emergency campaign materials (flags, billboards, banners, posters) to 7 Amber-rated DUNs. Close the visibility gap between PH and BN machinery. Restore BGU morale by demonstrating resource commitment. Convert "show of force" into voter confidence and swing factor activation.

### USE CASE-11: BN MANIFESTO COUNTER-MESSAGING

**PIR:** NS-11 (Opposition Strategy), NS-14 (Dominant Narratives)
**Decision Owner:** Opposition Analysis Cell
**Trigger:** BN manifesto launch 24 Jul at DUN Linggi + Pertang. 50k jobs promise vs PH's 30k. Zahid claims "game changer." No ground-level data on voter reception. This is an anticipated event — the counter-messaging must be ready to deploy within hours of the launch.

**Evidence Base:** Anticipated event based on confirmed BN manifesto launch schedule (daily brief confirms 24 Jul at Linggi + Pertang). BN messaging known: 50k jobs, "game changer" claim by Zahid. Confidence: HIGH (event is confirmed; messaging content is anticipated based on BN public statements).

**Timeline Constraint:** Counter-messaging deployed within 12 hours of BN manifesto launch — by 25 Jul 12:00 (T-7). Sustained through 31 Jul.

**Operational Objective:** Counter BN's manifesto messaging with factual, comparative PH messaging. Prevent the 50k jobs promise from becoming a dominant narrative. Expose BN manifesto gaps and credibility problems. Ensure PH's own 30k jobs promise is defended and contextualised.

---

**Phase 1: COLLECTION (12 hours — 24 Jul, day of launch)**

1. Deploy 2 audit teams to N.32 Linggi and N.02 Pertang for BN manifesto launch day (24 Jul). Capture: (a) crowd size and composition, (b) voter reactions to 50k jobs promise, (c) BN-PN joint framing (are they presenting a unified manifesto?), (d) specific manifesto pledges and how they are being communicated, (e) Zahid's "game changer" claim and audience response.
2. Monitor BN social media and news coverage of the manifesto launch in real-time.
3. Collect the full BN manifesto document — identify all pledges, costs, and feasibility issues.
4. Who: Opposition Analysis Cell audit teams (2 teams of 2) + media monitoring team (2).
5. Where: N.32 Linggi (manifesto launch venue), N.02 Pertang (co-launch venue), online monitoring.

**Phase 2: ANALYSIS (12 hours — 24–25 Jul)**

1. Assess BN manifesto strengths and vulnerabilities:
   - 50k jobs vs PH 30k: is the BN promise credible? What is the funding mechanism? Can it be delivered by a state government? Compare with PH's track record on job creation.
   - Other key pledges: identify promises that are (a) undeliverable, (b) contradictory, (c) copied from PH, (d) genuinely appealing to voters.
2. Decision criteria:
   - If 50k jobs promise is gaining traction with voters → deploy comparative messaging: PH has a track record; BN has promises without a plan.
   - If 50k jobs promise is seen as unrealistic → reinforce voter scepticism with factual analysis.
   - If BN-PN are presenting a unified manifesto → highlight the opportunistic nature of the BN-PN alliance.
   - If BN manifesto contains undeliverable promises → expose specific gaps.
3. Identify the 3 most effective counter-messages:
   - Counter 1: "50k jobs — di mana duit? BN tak ada pelan kewangan." (Where's the money? BN has no financial plan.)
   - Counter 2: "PH 30k jobs = rekod membuktikan. BN 50k jobs = janji tanpa bukti." (PH 30k = proven record. BN 50k = promise without evidence.)
   - Counter 3: "BN governance record: 1MDB, rasuah, hutang negeri. Boleh percaya?" (BN governance record: 1MDB, corruption, state debt. Can you trust them?)

**Phase 3: OPERATIONAL DEPLOYMENT (24–48 hours — 25–26 Jul)**

1. **Track A (Ground):** Deploy counter-messaging teams to N.32 Linggi and N.02 Pertang within 24 hours of BN launch. Equip BGU with talking points: "50k jobs — di mana duit? PH ada rekod, BN ada janji." Target: FELDA settlements, semi-urban localities, youth gatherings.
2. **Track B (Digital):** Deploy comparative infographic: PH 30k jobs (with track record) vs BN 50k jobs (without plan). Deploy on TikTok, WhatsApp, Facebook within 12 hours of BN launch. Target: all NS DUNs.
3. **Track C (Ceramah):** Schedule PH ceramah in N.32 Linggi and N.02 Pertang within 48 hours of BN launch. Candidate + senior PH leader directly address BN manifesto: "Janji vs Bukti." Deploy Aminuddin or a federal minister.
4. **Track D (Media):** Issue press statement from PH communications team within 12 hours: factual analysis of BN manifesto feasibility. Highlight undeliverable promises and financial gaps.
5. Who: Opposition Analysis Cell coordinates; BGU teams execute Track A; digital team executes Track B; candidate scheduling executes Track C; State Communications Director executes Track D.
6. Where: N.32 Linggi, N.02 Pertang (priority); all NS DUNs for digital and media tracks.

**Phase 4: VERIFICATION & FEEDBACK (26–31 Jul)**

1. Deploy auditors to N.32 Linggi and N.02 Pertang 48 hours after counter-messaging deployment.
2. Measure: % of voters who recall BN's 50k jobs promise; % who recall PH's counter-messaging; whether BN manifesto narrative is strengthening or weakening; whether PH's 30k jobs promise is better understood.
3. Feedback loop: Results feed to Opposition Analysis Cell and Narrative Intelligence Lead. If BN manifesto narrative is gaining ground, intensify Track A and Track B in affected DUNs.
4. Monitor for BN-PN joint messaging evolution post-manifesto — track whether BN and PN are coordinating on manifesto promises or diverging.
5. Report to State Intelligence Director in daily brief.

**Decision Point:** Opposition Analysis Cell decides by 25 Jul 12:00 (12 hours after BN launch) whether to activate all 4 tracks or concentrate resources based on voter reception analysis from Phase 1.

**Success Criteria:**
- BN 50k jobs promise does not become the dominant campaign narrative by 28 Jul.
- 40%+ of voters in N.32 Linggi and N.02 Pertang have heard PH's counter-messaging by 29 Jul.
- PH's 30k jobs promise is contextualised with track record — voters understand the difference between PH's proven record and BN's uncosted promise.
- BN manifesto feasibility gaps exposed in at least 2 media reports by 27 Jul.
- No DUN shows sentiment shift toward BN specifically due to the 50k jobs promise by 30 Jul.

**Escalation Path:** If BN's 50k jobs promise gains dominant narrative status in 3+ DUNs by 27 Jul → escalate to State Intelligence Director → emergency statewide ceramah tour with Aminuddin and federal ministers directly addressing jobs promise; deploy paid media campaign. If BN-PN joint manifesto messaging proves highly effective → escalate to State Communications Director → coordinated multi-party (PH component parties) response. Escalation flag: ESC-BNM-11.

**Resource Requirements:**
- Personnel: Opposition Analysis Cell (4 auditors for launch day); BGU counter-messaging teams (2 DUNs × 6 workers = 12); digital team (3); ceramah logistics team (2).
- Materials: Counter-messaging talking points (Malay-language, 1-page); comparative infographic; press statement; ceramah materials.
- Funding: Auditor transport (4 auditors × 1 day); counter-messaging material printing; digital content production; ceramah logistics (2 events).

### USE CASE-12: EMERGENCY T1 SEAT AUDIT DEPLOYMENT
