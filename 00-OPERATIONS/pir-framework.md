## 1. Mission

The Negeri Sembilan PRN Priority Intelligence Requirements framework establishes the critical intelligence questions that must be answered to support:

- State campaign leadership
- Political decision-makers
- Election and constituency war rooms
- Candidate and campaign machinery
- Strategic communications
- Stakeholder engagement
- Field mobilisation
- Electoral risk management

**Operating Structure:** State → Parliament → DUN → Daerah Mengundi → Locality → Community Cluster

**Primary Purpose:** Convert political data, field observations and public information into timely operational decisions.

## 3. Command Intelligence Questions

The command centre must continuously answer five fundamental questions:

1. **Where** are we electorally secure, competitive, vulnerable or deteriorating?
2. **Which** voter, locality, stakeholder or operational factors are driving the condition?
3. **What** changes have occurred since the previous assessment?
4. **What action** can realistically improve the position?
5. **Which** development requires immediate leadership escalation?

**Operational Principle:** Every intelligence product must support at least one defined operational decision.

## 5. Strategic PIRs (NS-01 to NS-03)

### NS-01: Statewide Electoral Position

**Priority Intelligence Question:**  
What is the current statewide electoral position across all 36 DUN seats, and which combination of constituencies is most likely to determine control of the State Legislative Assembly?

**Decisions Supported:**
- Statewide resource allocation
- Leadership deployment
- Campaign budget prioritisation
- Identification of defensive and offensive constituencies
- Determination of the minimum viable pathway to a governing majority

**DUN Classification System:**

| Classification | Operational Meaning |
|----------------|---------------------|
| **Secure** | Strong position with no immediate material threat |
| **Favourable** | Leading, but continued campaign activity is required |
| **Competitive** | Outcome remains genuinely contestable |
| **Vulnerable** | Position is deteriorating or opponent holds an advantage |
| **Critical** | Immediate intervention is necessary |
| **Insufficient Intelligence** | Available evidence is inadequate for classification |

**Escalation Triggers:**
- Projected governing pathway falls below 19 seats
- Three or more favourable seats move into competitive category
- A critical seat records sustained deterioration across two reporting cycles
- Statewide turnout assumptions materially change

---

### NS-02: Post-Dissolution Political Realignment

**Priority Intelligence Question:**  
How have the events leading to the dissolution affected voter confidence, party loyalty, coalition relationships and stakeholder alignment?

**Key Intelligence Requirements:**
- Public understanding of the political crisis
- Attribution of responsibility
- Perceived legitimacy of competing political positions
- Effect on support for incumbent and challenger parties
- Reaction among traditional party supporters
- Reaction among undecided and low-information voters
- Views of community, institutional and local leadership
- Whether the issue remains active or is being replaced by economic and local concerns

**Escalation Trigger:** Escalate when the issue produces measurable support movement across multiple DUNs, causes campaign partners or local machinery to disengage, creates conflicting public positions among senior leaders, or becomes the dominant statewide election narrative.

---

### NS-03: Government Formation Scenarios

**Priority Intelligence Question:**  
What are the most credible government-formation scenarios following polling day, including majority, minority, coalition and hung-assembly possibilities?

**Required Scenarios:**
1. Clear majority for one coalition
2. Narrow majority (19-20 seats)
3. No coalition reaching 19 seats (hung assembly)
4. Independent or minor-party representatives becoming decisive
5. Recounts, disputed results or delayed declarations affecting control
6. Post-election realignment attempts

**Analytical Requirement:** Each scenario must include probability, seat pathway, critical constituencies, political implications, legal and institutional considerations, recommended preparation, and key warning indicators.

## 6. Electoral and Constituency PIRs (NS-04 to NS-06)

### NS-04: DUN-Level Electoral Risk

**Priority Intelligence Question:**  
Which DUN constituencies are most likely to change political control, and what factors will determine the result?

**Assessment Dimensions:**
- Previous election result and winning margin
- Turnout history and electorate growth
- Demographic composition
- Urban, semi-urban or rural character
- Local economic conditions
- Candidate acceptance and incumbent performance
- Party machinery and opposition presence
- Local stakeholder influence and issue salience
- Field-contact performance

**Required Output:** One-page DUN intelligence profile with electoral classification, trend direction, top three political drivers, top three operational vulnerabilities, key localities, priority stakeholder groups, candidate assessment, opposition assessment, turnout forecast, recommended intervention, and confidence rating.

---

### NS-05: Daerah Mengundi and Locality Prioritisation

**Priority Intelligence Question:**  
Which Daerah Mengundi and localities offer the greatest opportunity for vote consolidation, persuasion or turnout improvement?

**Priority Segmentation:**

| Segment | Required Response |
|---------|-------------------|
| **Base Consolidation** | Protect established support and maximise turnout |
| **Persuasion Opportunity** | Focus lawful, issue-based engagement on undecided communities |
| **Turnout Risk** | Resolve participation barriers and machinery weaknesses |
| **Opponent Stronghold** | Maintain visibility and identify realistic vote-growth opportunities |
| **Rapidly Changing Locality** | Increase collection and field validation |
| **Operational Blind Spot** | Deploy immediate intelligence collection |

---

### NS-06: Turnout and Mobilisation Risk

**Priority Intelligence Question:**  
Which constituencies, polling districts and voter groups are at highest risk of reduced turnout, and what lawful interventions can remove participation barriers?

**Election-Day Triggers:**
- Turnout below the expected hourly baseline
- Priority streams recording abnormal delays
- Significant transport or access disruption
- Repeated voter confusion at specific polling centres
- Critical localities underperforming against the mobilisation target

## 7. Voter and Issue PIRs (NS-07 to NS-09)

### NS-07: Voter Sentiment and Support Movement

**Priority Intelligence Question:**  
Which issues are increasing or reducing support across Negeri Sembilan, and where is the movement concentrated?

**Core Issue Areas:**
- Cost of living, employment and wages
- Housing affordability
- Infrastructure and road conditions
- Water supply and utility reliability
- Flood mitigation
- Healthcare access
- Education and youth opportunities
- Rural and agricultural development
- Small-business conditions
- Public-service delivery
- Local-government performance
- State leadership and political stability

**Escalation Trigger:** An issue causes sustained negative movement across three or more constituencies; a local service-delivery problem becomes a statewide narrative; an issue begins affecting core supporters; existing messaging does not improve sentiment after two assessment cycles.

---

### NS-08: Undecided and Persuadable Electorate

**Priority Intelligence Question:**  
Where are undecided voters concentrated, what issues influence their decision, and which lawful public-engagement approaches are most likely to address their concerns?

**Operational Application:** Develop public policy explanations, open community engagement, candidate issue briefings, constituency-level communication, fact-based rebuttals, and service-delivery follow-through.

**Restriction:** The framework must not support covert manipulation, intimidation, discriminatory targeting or the exploitation of sensitive personal data.

---

### NS-09: Youth and First-Time Voter Participation

**Priority Intelligence Question:**  
What political, economic and social issues are shaping participation among younger and first-time voters?

**Intelligence Focus:** Employment and income, education and skills, housing, transportation, digital economy opportunities, governance and integrity, political relevance, candidate authenticity, preferred communication channels, turnout barriers.

**Required Action:** Develop public-facing, policy-centred engagement rather than personality-only or emotionally manipulative communications.

## 8. Candidate and Party PIRs (NS-10 to NS-11)

### NS-10: Candidate Suitability and Acceptance

**Priority Intelligence Question:**  
Which candidates strengthen or weaken electoral competitiveness within each DUN?

**Candidate Assessment Dimensions:**
- Local acceptance and name recognition
- Integrity and credibility
- Constituency presence and service record
- Political experience and communication ability
- Issue knowledge and grassroots relationships
- Demographic and community reach
- Campaign discipline
- Vulnerability to legitimate public criticism

**Escalation Trigger:** Candidate rejection threatens a competitive seat; local machinery refuses to support the candidate; a verified issue creates serious legal, reputational or ethical exposure; candidate messaging conflicts with state campaign direction.

---

### NS-11: Opposition Strategy and Campaign Activity

**Priority Intelligence Question:**  
What are opposing parties attempting to achieve, where are they concentrating resources, and which activities are materially improving their position?

**Collection Areas:** Candidate announcements, manifestos and policy positions, public campaign events, leadership visits, constituency deployments, advertising themes, public endorsements, volunteer mobilisation, local stakeholder engagement, lawful public digital communications, issue ownership, coalition or seat-allocation arrangements.

**Restriction:** Collection must be limited to lawful public information, authorised field reporting and legitimate political engagement. It must not involve unauthorised access, surveillance of private communications or deceptive acquisition of personal information.

## 9. Stakeholder PIRs (NS-12 to NS-13)

### NS-12: Stakeholder Alignment

**Priority Intelligence Question:**  
Which state, district and community stakeholders can materially influence electoral confidence, mobilisation or political legitimacy?

**Stakeholder Categories:**
- Traditional and customary institutions (Undangs)
- Community leaders
- Religious organisations
- Business associations
- Agricultural and rural groups
- Civil-society organisations
- Youth and student bodies
- Women's organisations
- Professional associations
- Local media
- Former political office-holders
- Influential local personalities

**Stakeholder Classification:**

| Position | Meaning |
|----------|---------|
| **Supportive** | Actively aligned or willing to assist |
| **Leaning Supportive** | Generally positive but not committed |
| **Neutral** | No discernible alignment |
| **Leaning Opposed** | Increasingly critical or disengaged |
| **Opposed** | Actively supporting an alternative position |
| **Unknown** | Insufficient verified information |

---

### NS-13: Adat, Institutional and Cultural Sensitivities

**Priority Intelligence Question:**  
What customary, institutional, cultural or local sensitivities could materially affect political legitimacy and voter perception?

**Intelligence Requirements:**
- Public understanding of Negeri Sembilan's customary governance traditions
- Community reaction to statements involving state institutions
- Local sensitivities associated with adat and traditional leadership
- Variation among districts and customary territories
- Risk of inaccurate, inflammatory or disrespectful campaign language
- Attempts to politicise institutional or cultural issues

**Required Response:** Immediate factual verification, legal and cultural review, disciplined spokesperson coordination, engagement through appropriate recognised channels, avoidance of speculative or inflammatory messaging.

## 10. Narrative and Information Environment PIRs (NS-14 to NS-15)

### NS-14: Dominant Election Narratives

**Priority Intelligence Question:**  
Which narratives currently shape voter interpretation of the election, and how are they moving across constituencies and communication channels?

**Narrative Categories:**
- Political stability and legitimacy
- State leadership and coalition consistency
- Economic performance and cost of living
- Service delivery and integrity
- Governance and institutional relationships
- Candidate credibility
- Local development

**Narrative Metrics:** Volume, velocity, sentiment, geographic reach, cross-platform spread, mainstream-media adoption, influencer amplification, persistence, audience response, offline validation.

**Escalation Trigger:** A misleading narrative achieves rapid statewide reach; a local incident becomes a state-level issue; official responses generate greater negative attention; a narrative materially alters candidate or party support.

---

### NS-15: Misinformation and Coordinated Influence Risk

**Priority Intelligence Question:**  
What false, misleading or coordinated information activity could disrupt informed electoral participation or public confidence?

**Indicators:**
- Coordinated repetition of identical claims
- Artificial engagement patterns
- Impersonation and manipulated images or video
- Fake announcements and false polling information
- Inaccurate candidate claims
- Incorrect voting dates or locations
- Threats intended to suppress participation

**Response Requirements:** Verify before classification, preserve evidence, assess reach and actual harm, coordinate factual correction, refer potential offences to authorised institutions, avoid amplifying low-reach content unnecessarily.

## 11. Campaign Machinery PIRs (NS-16 to NS-17)

### NS-16: Machinery Readiness

**Priority Intelligence Question:**  
Which DUN and Daerah Mengundi machinery units are ready, partially ready or non-operational?

**Readiness Rating:**

| Rating | Condition |
|--------|-----------|
| **Green** | Fully operational |
| **Amber** | Operational with material gaps |
| **Red** | Not ready or unable to meet minimum requirements |
| **Grey** | Readiness cannot be verified |

**Escalation Trigger:** A competitive DUN is rated red; more than 20% of Daerah Mengundi units fail minimum readiness; polling-agent coverage remains incomplete; field reporting is repeatedly absent or unreliable; election-day logistics remain unresolved after the defined deadline.

---

### NS-17: Data Readiness and Intelligence Coverage

**Priority Intelligence Question:**  
Is the campaign operating with accurate, complete, current and lawfully processed electoral information?

**Required Checks:**
- Voter-roll currency and DUN/Daerah Mengundi mapping
- Locality-code consistency and duplicate records
- Contact-data provenance and consent/lawful-use basis
- Field-update completeness and dashboard accuracy
- User access control and audit logging
- Incident-response procedures and data-retention requirements

**Critical Rule:** Personal data must not be sourced from breaches, unlawful databases or unauthorised disclosure. Sensitive personal information must not be used to infer or manipulate an individual's political beliefs.

## 13. Daily Political Intelligence Brief Structure

Each daily brief should contain:

### A. Executive Assessment (Maximum 5 Points)
- Current electoral position
- Material change since the previous report
- Highest-priority risk
- Highest-value opportunity
- Immediate leadership decision required

### B. Statewide Seat Dashboard
- Secure | Favourable | Competitive | Vulnerable | Critical | Insufficient Intelligence

### C. Critical DUN Updates
For each priority DUN: Current status, trend, key driver, opposition action, recommended response, owner, deadline.

### D. Narrative and Issue Environment
Top emerging narratives, dominant voter issues, geographic concentration, severity, required communications action.

### E. Machinery Readiness
Red and amber DUNs, unresolved operational gaps, field-reporting compliance, resource requirements.

### F. Stakeholder Developments
Position changes, priority engagements, unresolved concerns, leadership interventions required.

### G. Early-Warning Register
Trigger, evidence, potential impact, confidence, escalation level.

### H. Command Decisions
Decision required, responsible authority, deadline, implementation owner, verification method.

## 14. Analytical Standards

### Evidence Classification

| Type | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Verified Fact** | Confirmed by multiple reliable sources |
| **Corroborated Reporting** | Multiple sources agree on key details |
| **Single-Source Reporting** | One credible source only |
| **Analytical Judgment** | Inference based on available evidence |
| **Forecast** | Projection based on trends and models |
| **Unverified Allegation** | Claim without credible confirmation |

### Confidence Ratings

| Rating | Standard |
|--------|----------|
| **High** | Multiple reliable and mutually reinforcing sources |
| **Moderate** | Credible evidence with identifiable information gaps |
| **Low** | Limited, conflicting or weakly validated information |

**Rule:** No assessment should report certainty where the evidence only supports probability.

## 15. Legal and Ethical Operating Controls

The PIR framework must operate within Malaysian electoral, communications, cybersecurity and personal-data requirements.

**Prohibited Activities:**
- Unlawful acquisition of personal data
- Use of breached or stolen databases
- Unauthorised access to systems or private communications
- Individual political profiling based on sensitive personal information
- Intimidation, coercion or voter suppression
- Impersonation and deceptive engagement
- Fabrication of public sentiment
- Defamatory or unverified allegations
- Automated manipulation of political discourse

**Permitted Use:** Political intelligence should be used to understand aggregate public concerns, improve lawful engagement, strengthen service delivery and support informed strategic decisions.

## 16. Operational Principle

The Negeri Sembilan PRN PIR framework must function as a **command-and-decision system** rather than a passive monitoring list.

**Every Active PIR Must Specify:**
1. The question to be answered
2. The political decision supported
3. The geographic scope
4. The required evidence
5. The analytical owner
6. The reporting deadline
7. The escalation threshold
8. The recommended lawful intervention
9. The expected outcome
10. The conditions for closure

**Effectiveness Measures:**
- Detects political change early
- Prioritises the constituencies that matter
- Improves leadership decisions
- Directs resources to measurable needs
- Strengthens campaign readiness
- Reduces operational blind spots
- Maintains legal, ethical and evidentiary discipline

| Tier | Status | Confidence | Deployment |
|------|--------|------------|------------|
| **VERIFIED** | Multi-source confirmed (SPR + 2+ news) | 95%+ | Deploy-ready |

**Standard:** 100% "absolute truth" — no single-source claims in operational briefs.
