SEREMBAN: The Perikatan Nasional (PN) Supreme Council has strong grounds to remove Bersatu from the coalition, said suspended Bersatu vice-president Datuk Seri Ronald Kiandee.

He said this followed Bersatu's decision to field candidates against PN partners in eight state seats in the Negri Sembilan election on Aug 1.

"This clearly shows that the party no longer wishes to remain with PN," he said in a Facebook post today.

Bersatu is set to face PAS in Serting, Ampangan, Paroi and Bagan Pinang, as well as Datuk Seri Hamzah Zainudin's Parti Wawasan Negara in Klawang, Sikamat, Mambau and Gemas.

The Election Commission has set Aug 1 as polling day, with early voting on July 28.
