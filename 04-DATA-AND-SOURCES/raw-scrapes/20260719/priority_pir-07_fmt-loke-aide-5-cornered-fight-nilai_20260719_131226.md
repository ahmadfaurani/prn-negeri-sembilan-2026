J Arul Kumar, who has represented Nilai since 2013, faces candidates from BN, Bersatu, Berjasa and an independent.

Arul, who has represented Nilai since 2013, is seeking a fourth consecutive term as its assemblyman.

He is being challenged by Barisan Nasional’s Lai Chien Kong, Bersatu’s V Saravan Kumar, Berjasa’s Zamani Ibrahim and independent candidate Omar Isa.

Nilai is a mixed seat where the majority of the electorate are Malay voters (42.5%), followed by Chinese (32.6%) and Indians (21.9%). It falls within the Seremban parliamentary constituency represented by Loke since 2013.

Arul won the Nilai seat with a whopping 10,889-vote majority in a straight fight with Perikatan Nasional during the 2023 state election, when PH and BN contested as allies.

This time around, BN is cooperating with PN while PH is going solo in all 36 seats.

The Negeri Sembilan polls will take place on Aug 1, with early voting set for July 28.

Another 5-cornered fight for PKR seat

There will be another five-cornered race in the PKR seat of Sri Tanjung, with Dr G Rajasekaran seeking a second term as its assemblyman.

The PH candidate has to contend with BN’s A Achutan, Bersatu’s M Leevineshwaran and two independent candidates, A Saravanan and Islah Wahyudi Zainudin.

Sri Tanjung, a mixed seat in the Port Dickson constituency, has a voter breakdown of 37.2% Malay, 31.5% Chinese, and 28.35% Indian.

Rajasekaran won the seat with a 3,996-vote majority in 2023.
