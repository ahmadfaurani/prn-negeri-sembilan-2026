The former three-term Negeri Sembilan menteri besar has represented Rantau for five terms since winning the seat in 2004.

The former Negeri Sembilan menteri besar is up against Pakatan Harapan’s Dr Azizul Hakim Mahdi for the seat which the Umno deputy president has represented for five terms since first winning it in 2004.

Mohamad, also known as Tok Mat, was accompanied by BN chairman Ahmad Zahid Hamidi as he submitted his nomination paper at Dewan Seri Rembau here.

He is among the highest-profile candidates contesting in the Aug 1 polls.

Rantau is a mixed seat in the Rembau parliamentary constituency, which Mohamad also represents in the Dewan Rakyat.

The Malay community makes up 54.8% of voters in Rantau, followed by the Indians (27.6%) and Chinese (16%).

Mohamad, who is also foreign minister, served as Negeri Sembilan menteri besar from 2004 to 2018.

He won the seat with a 10,280-vote majority in a straight fight with Perikatan Nasional in the 2023 state polls, when BN and PH contested as allies.

This time around, BN is cooperating with PN while PH is going solo in all 36 seats.

The Negeri Sembilan polls has been fixed for Aug 1, with early voting taking place on July 28.

Three-way fights in Paroi and Kota

Two other seats in Rembau – Paroi and Kota – will see three-cornered contests involving BN, PH and Bersatu candidates.

Paroi has changed hands between Umno, PAS and Amanah since 2004 but was won by PAS’s Kamarul Ridzuan Zin in the 2023 state election by a majority of 5,539 votes.

Kamarul is now seeking a second term and has to contend with PH’s Ahmad Shahir Shah and Bersatu’s Nazree Yunus.

Meanwhile, the Kota seat will involve former assemblyman Suhaimi Aini of BN, PH’s Allif Ibrahim and Bersatu’s Akmal Noradzmi Abd Rahim.

Kota has never left Umno’s grasp since the constituency was created in 1959, though Suhaimi won the seat for BN with a very slim 135-vote majority in 2023.

There will also be a straight fight in Chembong between BN’s Zaifulbahri Idris and PH’s Danish Nazran Murad.

Zaifulbahri has represented Chembong since 2008 and retained the seat with a 4,335-vote majority in 2023.
