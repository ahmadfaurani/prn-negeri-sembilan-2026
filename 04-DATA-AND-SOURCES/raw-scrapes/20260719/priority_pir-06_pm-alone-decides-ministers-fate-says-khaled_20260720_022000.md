KUALA PILAH: The appointment and tenure of ministers rest solely with the prime minister, Defence Minister Datuk Seri Mohamed Khaled Nordin said. He said this in response to Foreign Minister Datuk Seri Mohamad Hasan's statement that he was prepared to relinquish his post if instructed to do so. "I believe, as I have said, that the appointment of ministers is entirely at the prime minister's discretion. "If people don't want you anymore, why wait?" he said after launching the Barisan Nasional (BN) Johol election machinery and introducing candidate Datuk Saiful Yazan Sulaiman here. Earlier, Mohamad said he was ready to step down if directed by Prime Minister Datuk Seri Anwar Ibrahim. He made the remark after Anwar warned ministers, deputy ministers and office-bearers in government agencies against using their positions to attack fellow members of the unity government while campaigning for the Negeri Sembilan state election. Meanwhile, Khaled said the Negri Sembilan election campaign should focus on state development issues rather than personal attacks. "This is a state election, so naturally the issues discussed should relate to the state and its future direction. "As emphasised by the Umno president (Datuk Seri Dr Ahmad Zahid Hamidi), who is also the BN chairman, the campaign should be conducted prudently and should not adopt approaches that hurt people's feelings or disparage others," he said. © New Straits Times Press (M) Bhd
