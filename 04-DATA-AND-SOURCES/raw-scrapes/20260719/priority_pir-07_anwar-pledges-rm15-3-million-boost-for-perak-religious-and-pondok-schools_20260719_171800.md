IPOH: The Madani government will continue to enhance and uplift religious and pondok schools nationwide, said Prime Minister Datuk Seri Anwar Ibrahim.He said pondok schools once played a vital role in providing education, but fell behind when rapid changes took place and they were unable to acquire knowledge in other fields.In this regard, Anwar said he was proud of the religious teachers, pondok school leaders and tok guru for preserving Islamic traditions and classical religious texts while being prepared to explore new disciplines such as computer technology and artificial intelligence (AI).He thanked the Malaysia Digital Economy Corporation (MDEC) and the Implementation Coordination Unit (ICU) under the Prime Minister's Department for their cooperation in helping to improve these institutions."I give my assurance, insya-Allah, as we continue to remain healthy and the economy remains like this, as I told Sheikh Wazir Che Awang Al-Makki, insya-Allah, we will enhance and uplift religious schools and pondok schools throughout our country," he said.Also present were Perak Menteri Besar Datuk Seri Saarani Mohamad, Minister in the Prime Minister's Department (Religious Affairs) Dr Zulkifli Hasan, and Chief Secretary to the Government Tan Sri Shamsul Azri Abu Bakar.The prime minister said the Madani government will continue the digital transformation initiative for Islamic educational institutions that was launched in March.He noted that several pondok schools and maahad, or religious secondary schools, had been equipped with new technologies to ensure students are prepared to acquire new knowledge."So, I appreciate the cooperation extended by these schools, and today we will continue to increase the allocations and assistance as announced," he said.Anwar advised students at the schools to study diligently and prove that they can compete not only in terms of intellect, competence and proficiency in specific disciplines such as syariah and Arabic, but also in emerging fields of knowledge.At the event, the prime minister announced that RM15 million had been approved for pondok and religious schools throughout Perak for 2026, while RM300,000 was approved specifically for Sekolah Agama Bantuan Kerajaan Maahad Al-Ummah in Chemor."We approved allocations for 115 pondok schools in Perak last year. This year, we are increasing the allocation by providing RM15 million for all pondok schools in Perak that did not receive sufficient assistance previously," he said.© New Straits Times Press (M) Bhd
