REMBAU: Foreign Minister Datuk Seri Mohamad Hasan says he is ready to step down from his position if ordered to by Prime Minister Datuk Seri Anwar Ibrahim. The Barisan Nasional deputy chairman said no BN member had criticised the federal government throughout the Negri Sembilan election period so far. "We have never done so, nor have we touched on personal matters. In our campaigning, we just want our message to get through to voters. "It is up to the prime minister as it is he who appoints the ministers and deputy ministers," he told reporters here. Mohamad, affectionately known as Tok Mat, was commenting on Anwar's warning to ministers, deputy ministers and government agency leaders not to use their positions to attack fellow unity government partners during state election campaigns. Anwar said earlier today anyone who does so should resign. Mohamad, who will be seeking to defend his Rantau seat in the upcoming Negri Sembilan election, described political statements as "normal" during election seasons. "I have not taken offence, but let's not get too excited too quickly. This is normal in elections, and it has happened in the past. "It doesn't mean we have to become personal enemies... certainly not. We simply want the people to understand and to give their support to BN. "We will continue to persuade them and make sure they are no longer taken in by slander and false allegations. Back in the 2018 general election, BN did not lose because of anything else... it lost because of excessive slander," he said. Mohamad reminded voters not to be taken in by slander, including those which come out in social media, which tend to pit Malaysians against each other. "Let's think rationally, and let's not resort to personal attacks when we campaign," he said. © New Straits Times Press (M) Bhd
