The MCA president says the two coalitions are working together with the aim of forming a stable Negeri Sembilan government.

He said the strategic cooperation was only aimed at avoiding multi-cornered contests that could split votes, and to improve their chances of forming a stable state government.

“This cooperation aims to establish a stable, efficient and effective government to ensure Negeri Sembilan’s continued development,” he told a press conference in Seremban.

“It must be emphasised that this cooperation does not amount to an alliance. Each party will continue to uphold its respective ideological principles while playing the role of checks and balances against one another.”

According to Wee, MCA’s involvement was to ensure the Chinese community, the country’s multiracial society, vernacular education and religious freedom continued to have representation in the government.

He also stressed that MCA would not provide unconditional support to any party.

MCA Youth secretary-general Saw Yee Fung had questioned whether the party’s leadership had endorsed BN’s cooperation with PAS for the Aug 1 polls.

She was later told by MCA secretary-general Chong Sin Woon in a text message that she need not participate in BN’s Negeri Sembilan election campaign.

BN is contesting 25 seats, while PN is vying for the remaining 11, with not a single seat overlap.

PAS president Abdul Hadi Awang said PAS had agreed in principle to allow BN to defend the 14 seats it previously held in Negeri Sembilan, while PAS would contest the three seats it won in the previous state polls.

Wee said the decision to participate in the cooperation was finalised in the early hours of July 15 after considering feedback from various parties, including the Negeri Sembilan MCA leadership.

“BN eventually decided to contest in 25 seats where it believes it has the best chances of winning,” he said.

According to Wee, the cooperation would also not prevent other parties from contesting, as this was a right guaranteed under the democratic system.

“So, with this understanding, this arrangement is what we call cooperation. It is not an alliance and it will not lead to the formation of a new party or coalition.

“I also must stress here that we recognise that in a vibrant democracy, any individual or party is free to contest,” he said.

Separately, Sin Chew reported MCA secretary-general Chong Sin Woon as saying the decision not to require Saw’s participation in the election campaign was a collective decision by the party and BN.

He said MCA and BN believed the incident had caused considerable damage and could also become an issue used by their opponents to attack them.

“As secretary-general, I am carrying out the task entrusted to me by the party. This was not my personal decision, and it certainly was not about bullying a young woman,” he was quoted as saying after the press conference.
