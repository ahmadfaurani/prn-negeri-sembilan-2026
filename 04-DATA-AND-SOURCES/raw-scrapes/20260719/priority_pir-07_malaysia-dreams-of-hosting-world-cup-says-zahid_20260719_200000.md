BANGI: Malaysia aspires to host the FIFA World Cup, the world's biggest football tournament, said Deputy Prime Minister Datuk Seri Dr Ahmad Zahid Hamidi. Ahmad Zahid, who is also Rural and Regional Development Minister, said he hoped to see Malaysia compete in the prestigious tournament as Southeast Asia's representative. "God willing, one day, we aspire for Malaysia to host the World Cup. "Hopefully, by then, the Malaysian team will be among the World Cup participants representing Southeast Asia," he said. He was speaking at a giant-screen viewing of the World Cup final between Argentina and Spain, held in conjunction with the Semarak Subuh programme at Bangi Square 2 here today. Harimau Malaya have climbed to 136th in the latest FIFA world rankings. According to the latest update, Malaysia have accumulated 1,086.22 points, moving up two places from 138th. Hundreds of football fans gathered at Bangi Square 2 to watch the final of the world's most prestigious football tournament between defending champions Argentina and Spain. Activities held in conjunction with the final included a "Borak Bola" session featuring national football legends Datuk Zainal Abidin Hassan, Mohd Farizal Marlias and Mohamad Zaquan Adha Abdul Radzak. The session was hosted by Hafizal Hamad. After the match, Ahmad Zahid is scheduled to join the Semarak Subuh programme at Surau Al-Hamidiah here to conclude the event. - Bernama © New Straits Times Press (M) Bhd
