Negeri Sembilan Umno chief Jalaluddin Alias (in black) participating in a tug-of-war game at the Kemas Skills Day programme in Felda Pasoh 4.
JELEBU: Perikatan Nasional (PN) has agreed that Barisan Nasional (BN) will fill the menteri besar’s post in Negeri Sembilan if the two coalitions succeed in forming the state government after the Aug 1 election, says Negeri Sembilan Umno chief Jalaluddin Alias.
Jalaluddin said no discussions had taken place on who would be named as menteri besar or how exco seats would be allocated.
“We never discussed who should become menteri besar. In fact, I would like to thank the PN leadership for declaring early that they would leave the menteri besar’s post to Umno and BN.
“We (BN and PN) have not discussed who the menteri besar candidate will be. Neither have we discussed the allocation of exco seats because our priority is to achieve victory first,” he told reporters after attending the Kemas Skills Day programme in Felda Pasoh 4.
Jalaluddin, who is also BN’s candidate for the Pertang seat, said the priority for now was to build a strong understanding between the two coalitions to secure victory before any negotiation on positions.
“Our goal is to win as many seats as possible first. Only then will we discuss positions that can strengthen the state government and not to satisfy any personal ambitions,” he said.
“We want to form an administration capable of governing the state well. If someone is not qualified for a position, we will not appoint them simply to fulfil a quota.”
Asked whether he was in contention for the menteri besar’s post, Jalaluddin said apart from himself, BN had several qualified leaders who could be considered, including Umno deputy president Mohamad Hasan and (Seri Menanti candidate) Sufian Maradzi.
“It is up to the BN chairman to decide. Don’t worry, we have many qualified leaders,” he said.
Negeri Sembilan will head to the polls on Aug 1, with 36 seats up for grabs.
BN and PN have an electoral understanding under which BN will be contesting 25 seats and PN 11, with no overlap.
