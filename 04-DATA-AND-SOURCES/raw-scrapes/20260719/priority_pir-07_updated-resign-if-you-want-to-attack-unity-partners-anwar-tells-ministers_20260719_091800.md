Title: [UPDATED] Resign if you want to attack unity partners, Anwar tells ministers

## Full text

IPOH: Prime Minister Datuk Seri Anwar Ibrahim has warned that ministers, deputy ministers and heads of federal agencies must resign if they use their positions to attack fellow unity government partners during state election campaigns.Anwar said while parties aligned at the federal level were free to contest against one another in state elections, those holding federal office must maintain discipline and not turn their positions into political platforms."If they wish to do so, they should first resign from their positions as ministers, deputy ministers or members of agency boards," he told reporters after attending the Madani Kita Programme at Dewan Komuniti Taman Perpaduan here today.He said the reminder was conveyed during discussions yesterday with Deputy Prime Ministers Datuk Seri Dr Ahmad Zahid Hamidi and Datuk Seri Fadillah Yusof following the ongoing Negri Sembilan election campaign."During my discussions with Datuk Seri Zahid and Datuk Seri Fadillah, we reminded candidates, particularly those holding positions as ministers, deputy ministers or heads of government agencies, that they may contest on state-related issues."But at the federal level, if they hold a position, they must not use it as a political platform to attack or contradict one another because they are also required to maintain discipline," he said.The Tambun member of Parliament said it was normal in a democratic system for parties working together at the federal level to face off in state elections."We know there are parties that are together at the federal level but are contesting against one another at the state level. That is acceptable in a democratic system, and everyone is entitled to their own interpretation," he said.However, Anwar added that candidates holding positions in the federal administration should limit their campaigning to state issues and refrain from attacking unity government partners using their official positions.The directive, he said, applied to all federal agencies, including the Federal Land Development Authority (Felda), Majlis Amanah Rakyat (Mara), the Rubber Industry Smallholders Development Authority (Risda) and others."I do not care which agency it is, whether it is Felda, Mara, Risda or any other agency under my purview. We must ensure that no one breaches this principle. If there is any violation, I will ask them to step down," Anwar said.He said federal office-bearers had been instructed to exercise restraint to preserve working relations within the unity government and ensure government business continued uninterrupted."For now, we have asked them to exercise restraint so that our relationship at the federal level, and possibly in states such as Perak, remains good, allowing government work to continue smoothly."If there is to be political competition, let it be confined to the state level," he said.Also present was Perak Menteri Besar Datuk Seri Saarani Mohamad.© New Straits Times Press (M) Bhd
