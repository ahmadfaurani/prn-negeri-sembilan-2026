France will take on England in the 2026 FIFA World Cup third-place playoff on Saturday (18 July), with kick-off set for 10 pm BST (5 pm ET) — that’s 5 am Sunday, 19 July, Malaysian time. This match is separate from the final — both France and England have already lost their semifinals and are out of contention for the title. The match, officially known as the “Match for Third Place,” decides which team finishes third and which finishes fourth at this year’s tournament. The third-place playoff has long struggled for relevance in football culture. A few reasons explain why: Both teams are playing just days after losing painful semifinal matches, often leaving squads mentally and physically drained. Unlike the final, there’s no trophy on the line — only a medal placing.
