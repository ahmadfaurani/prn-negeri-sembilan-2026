## Full text

Suspended party vice-president Ronald Kiandee points out that more than half of the council's members have either resigned or been suspended or sacked.

Kiandee said Bersatu president Muhyiddin Yassin’s claim that the party’s internal crisis would be resolved internally did not address the fundamental issue of whether the Supreme Council could still function under the party’s constitution.

Citing Clause 13.12 of Bersatu’s constitution, which requires more than half of the council’s members to be present for a valid meeting, Kiandee provided a list of the party’s 49-member Supreme Council, which he claimed showed that 27 members had either quit the party, resigned from their posts, been sacked or suspended.

“This clearly shows that Muhyiddin has failed to manage the party’s internal crisis. The failure to prove that the quorum can be met will raise serious questions over the validity of every decision made by the Supreme Council,” he said in a statement.

Kiandee warned that Bersatu risked being placed in an administrative limbo, and would not be able to make decisions that were valid under its constitution, if the council no longer had sufficient quorum.

He also said the party’s internal turmoil was “deeply embarrassing” and could affect Bersatu’s standing as a political party if the matter required intervention from the Registrar of Societies.

The crisis could also disrupt Perikatan Nasional’s administration and strategic planning, particularly ahead of the Negeri Sembilan state election, he said.

Kiandee called on the PN Supreme Council to convene immediately to assess Bersatu’s position within the coalition and consider steps to ensure PN’s stability and credibility were not affected by the crisis faced by Bersatu.

“The interests of Perikatan Nasional as a political coalition that represents the hopes of millions of Malaysians must take precedence over the interests of any individual,” he said.

“A prolonged crisis without a resolution would undermine public confidence in PN’s ability to provide stable, principled and credible leadership.”

Numerous Bersatu members and top officials have been sacked or suspended over the past few months for breaching the party’s constitution and its code of conduct and ethics.

Some of them claimed they were given the boot because of their close ties with former Bersatu deputy president Hamzah Zainudin, who was sacked from the party in February at the height of a leadership tussle with Muhyiddin.
