IPOH: People must ensure the Jalur Gemilang is displayed correctly, especially when they use artificial intelligence to produce content featuring the national flag, says the communications minister. Datuk Seri Fahmi Fadzil said every detail of the flag, including its 14 stripes, must be represented accurately. "We encourage Malaysians taking part in the Jalur Gemilang campaign to spend time learning the procedures and etiquette in flying the flag. "At the same time, everyone must be careful when generating content using AI so that there are no mistakes or shortcomings," he said after attending the National Month and Fly The Jalur Gemilang 2026 programme at the Sultan Azlan Shah Health Ministry Training Institute here yesterday. The event was launched by Prime Minister Datuk Seri Anwar Ibrahim. Fahmi said the ministry would work with the Malaysian Press Institute and the Malaysian Media Council to engage media organisations in ensuring representations of the Jalur Gemilang were accurate. He encouraged people to start flying the flag from now until at least Sept 16. Various programmes would be organised, including the Jelajah Jago dan Merdeka initiative. "These programmes will later be compiled on the Merdeka 360 website," Fahmi said. He hoped the National Month curtain-raiser served as more than just a ceremonial opening. "This event should ignite the spirit of patriotism. It should help us not only to remember the history of our independence and the formation of Malaysia, but also give meaning to the role we play as citizens in safeguarding sovereignty, preserving unity and securing the future of our country." On the action that would be taken against those who incorrectly display or produce inaccurate representations of the Jalur Gemilang, Fahmi said the government's initial approach would be to provide advice. © New Straits Times Press (M) Bhd
