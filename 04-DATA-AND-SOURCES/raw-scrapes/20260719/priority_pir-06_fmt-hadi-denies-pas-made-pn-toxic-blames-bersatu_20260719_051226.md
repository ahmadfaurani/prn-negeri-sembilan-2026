The PAS president says ties with Bersatu soured due to alleged misconduct by Muhyiddin Yassin's party within PN.

Instead, Hadi blamed Bersatu for the deterioration in ties between the two parties.

“That is not true. The rift was caused by Bersatu’s misconduct within PN,” he told reporters at the Jempol district administration complex during the nomination process for the Serting, Palong, Jeram Padang and Bahau state seats.

Yesterday, Muhyiddin accused PAS of making baseless allegations and spreading defamatory claims against Bersatu leaders in an attempt to sideline the party.

Citing the political crisis in Perlis, Muhyiddin said it was caused by mistakes made by PAS leaders themselves. The episode saw the menteri besar’s post change hands from PAS to Bersatu.

On June 8, PAS severed political ties with Bersatu. However, both parties remained in PN and used the same logo for the Johor state polls.

On Bersatu’s plan to form a new coalition after the Negeri Sembilan state election, Hadi said the matter was up to his former ally, adding that PN wanted to focus on cooperation aimed at “saving the people”.

Asked about the understanding between Barisan Nasional (BN) and PN for the Negeri Sembilan polls, Hadi said PAS grassroots members had responded positively to the arrangement, and efforts would be made to expand cooperation nationwide.

“We will work towards expanding cooperation nationwide. What is important is that our country remains safe,” he said.

BN and PN have an electoral understanding for the Aug 1 polls, under which BN will contest 25 and PN 11.

Bersatu, however, has broken away from the coalition and is contesting 24 seats under its own logo.
