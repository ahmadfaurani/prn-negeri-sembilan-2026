JELEBU: Negri Sembilan Umno liaison committee chairman Datuk Seri Jalaluddin Alias has questioned accusations by several Pakatan Harapan (PH) leaders that he committed an act of treachery.Jalaluddin, who is the Barisan Nasional (BN) candidate for the Pertang seat, said his actions, along with those of his colleagues, merely involved withdrawing support for Negri Sembilan Menteri Besar Datuk Seri Aminuddin Harun.He said the accusations were illogical."Some PH leaders said I had committed treachery (derhaka). I withdrew my support for the menteri besar, but who exactly was I being treacherous towards? Was I being treacherous towards the menteri besar?"Having differences with or withdrawing support for the menteri besar cannot be described as disloyalty. Treachery refers to disloyalty towards the ruler or the Undang. I merely withdrew my support for the menteri besar," he said.He said this after attending the Jelebu Division Community Development Department Skills Day Programme at Felda Pasoh 4 today.The Jelebu member of Parliament said the matter needed to be understood by the people and urged PH leaders not to twist the issue."Do not spin the issue. I have said this many times — do not instil hatred among the people over the weaknesses that we have created."Times have changed, and the people are more mature now," he said.Touching on an incident during nomination yesterday, when he was booed and jeered by PH supporters, Jalaluddin also questioned their behaviour."Yesterday, during the announcement in Jelebu, when the PH candidate was announced, I applauded. Why did I applaud? Was I supporting the PH candidate? No. I was respecting and recognising the democratic process and the right of any party to contest."But when my name was announced as the Pertang candidate, they booed and jeered at me. Is this the attitude and mentality of the people today?"So they should reflect on that themselves. But it is alright, in a way, it is good. The people can decide who is worthy of their votes and who is not," he said.© New Straits Times Press (M) Bhd
