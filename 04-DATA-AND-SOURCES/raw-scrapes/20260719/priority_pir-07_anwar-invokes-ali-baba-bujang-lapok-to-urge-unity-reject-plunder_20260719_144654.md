Title: Anwar invokes Ali Baba Bujang Lapok to urge unity, reject plunder

## Full text

IPOH: Malaysians must unite with a clear purpose to improve the lives of the people and strengthen the nation, rather than exploiting the country's resources for personal gain.Using an analogy from the popular P. Ramlee film Ali Baba Bujang Lapok, Prime Minister Datuk Seri Anwar Ibrahim said unity alone was not enough if it was used for the wrong reasons."If we want to begin with unity, what is the purpose? Being united is like in the P. Ramlee film Ali Baba Bujang Lapok, where the 40 thieves were also united."Some people may not like hearing me say this because some will say, 'We must unite.' But unite for what? We must unite to improve the quality of life of the people. Not unite to steal or plunder timber and wealth," he said.Anwar said this in his speech at the launch of the National Month and Fly the Jalur Gemilang celebrations at the Sultan Azlan Shah Health Ministry Training Institute here today.Also present were Communications Minister Datuk Seri Fahmi Fadzil and Perak Menteri Besar Datuk Seri Saarani Mohamad.He said unity must be rooted in values, integrity, and responsibility, especially as Malaysia moved towards becoming a more advanced nation driven by technology, innovation, and sustainable development.Anwar said the true meaning of independence went beyond flying the national flag, as it represented the sacrifices, struggles and aspirations of past generations.He said Malaysians must remember that independence was achieved after a long and difficult journey involving immense sacrifices by those who fought against colonial rule."The combination of these three, blood, sweat and tears, was what produced independence," he said.Anwar added that younger generations must understand the realities faced by their ancestors, who lived under colonial rule where their dignity, rights and control over national affairs were limited.He said the country's resources, economic system, education and administration were once controlled by foreign powers, with decisions made based on their own interests."That is the meaning of colonisation. We lost our identity. We had no right to voice our opinions. We had no opportunity to determine our own administrative systems and other matters," he said.He said the history of colonisation, including events such as the Pangkor Treaty and the earlier Portuguese conquest of Melaka, should serve as a reminder of the importance of protecting the nation's sovereignty.He said decisions affecting the country were once made thousands of kilometres away by foreign governments without taking into account the culture, religion and history of the local people."Imagine the fate of this country at one time, when it was governed by a region thousands of miles away. It was not based on our culture, our religion or our history," he said.Anwar said the struggle for independence was not achieved by one community alone, but through the collective strength of Malays, Chinese, Indians, Dayak, Kadazan and other communities.He said the contributions of all communities must be acknowledged as part of Malaysia's history."Could independence have been achieved without the participation of the Chinese, Indians and other communities? No," he said.He said this was why the country's founding leaders, led by Malaysia's first Prime Minister Tunku Abdul Rahman Putra Al-Haj, chose "Bersekutu Bertambah Mutu" as the national motto.Anwar said the motto reflected the principle that Malaysia's strength came from unity despite differences in race, religion, culture and region."We unite because we are different. Different races, different religions, different cultures and different regions. But when we work together and unite, our quality and strength increase," he said.He said Malaysians should not allow differences to be manipulated by irresponsible parties to create division.Anwar questioned why issues related to race and identity continued to dominate discussions despite Malaysia having achieved independence for almost 70 years and Sabah and Sarawak being part of Malaysia for six decades."Instead of discussing how to elevate the dignity, status and well-being of the people and the country, we are still busy talking about skin colour and physical differences," he said.He said the country's focus should instead be on creating opportunities, strengthening the economy and improving the quality of life of all Malaysians.Anwar said the Malaysia Madani framework was aimed at building a nation that was not only economically successful but also guided by strong values.He said Malaysia must continue progressing in strategic areas, including semiconductor manufacturing, quantum computing, artificial intelligence and green technology.He added that Perak had the potential to become a major contributor to the country's economic transformation.Anwar said the state, which was once known mainly for tin mining and timber resources, was now seeing growth in new industries.He highlighted the Kerian Integrated Green Industrial Park (KIGIP) project as a potential catalyst in turning Kerian into a technology and green industry hub."KIGIP could become a new technology centre focusing on green technology. Hopefully, if successful, it will become one of the leading centres in the region. From where? From Kerian," he said.He added that national development must always focus on raising the dignity, status and well-being of Malaysians."We want this country to become a great and successful nation," he said.As the country prepares to celebrate National Month, Anwar reminded Malaysians that the Jalur Gemilang should represent more than just a symbol of patriotism."It is not merely about flying the Jalur Gemilang. It is about flying it with pride," he said.He urged Malaysians, especially the younger generation, to preserve the spirit of unity and continue building a Malaysia that remains independent, sovereign and respected globally.© New Straits Times Press (M) Bhd
