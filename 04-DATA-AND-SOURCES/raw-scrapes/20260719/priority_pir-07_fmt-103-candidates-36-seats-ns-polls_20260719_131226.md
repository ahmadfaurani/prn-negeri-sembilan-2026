The polls will see 11 straight fights, 21 three-cornered contests and four multi-cornered battles.

EC chairman Ramlan Harun said at a press conference that the election will see contests in all 36 state seats, with both the government and opposition blocs fielding candidates statewide.

Pakatan Harapan and Barisan Nasional have fielded 36 and 25 candidates respectively, followed by 24 from Bersatu, 11 from Perikatan Nasional, and one each from Parti Sosialis Malaysia, Parti Orang Asli Malaysia and Parti Berjasa.

Four independents are also taking part in the election.

The full list comprises 94 men and nine women.

The polls will see 11 straight fights, 21 three-cornered contests, two four-cornered contests and two five-cornered contests.

The four-cornered contests will take place in Jeram Padang and Rahang, while the five-cornered contests will be in Nilai and Sri Tanjung.

The oldest candidate is PH’s Abd Latif A Tambi in Gemencheh, aged 70, while the youngest candidate is Bersatu’s M Leevineshwaran in Sri Tanjung, aged 23.

A total of 889,490 Negeri Sembilan voters are eligible to cast their ballots. Early voting will be held on July 28, while polling day is on Aug 1.
