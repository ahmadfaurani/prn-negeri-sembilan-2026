Title: Fahmi: Be cautious when using AI to generate content featuring Jalur Gemilang

## Full text

IPOH: Communications Minister Datuk Seri Fahmi Fadzil has reminded Malaysians to ensure the Jalur Gemilang is displayed correctly, especially when using artificial intelligence (AI) to generate content involving the national flag.While the use of AI brought many benefits, Fahmi said the public must remain cautious to avoid inaccuracies when producing images or materials featuring the national flag.He said every detail of the Jalur Gemilang, including its 14 stripes, must be represented accurately as it carried significant meaning."We also encourage Malaysians taking part in the flying Jalur Gemilang campaign to spend some time learning about the correct procedures and etiquette in flying the Jalur Gemilang."At the same time, everyone must be careful when generating content using AI so that there are no mistakes or shortcomings. The 14 stripes on the Jalur Gemilang are very important," he said.Fahmi said this to reporters after attending the National Month and Fly Jalur Gemilang 2026 programme at the Sultan Azlan Shah Health Ministry Training Institute here today.The event was launched by Prime Minister Datuk Seri Anwar Ibrahim.He said the ministry would also work with the Malaysian Press Institute (MPI) and the Malaysian Media Council to engage media organisations in ensuring representations of the Jalur Gemilang, particularly those created using AI, were accurate throughout the National Month celebrations.He encouraged the public to start flying the Jalur Gemilang from now until at least Sept 16 in conjunction with the National Day and Malaysia Day celebrations.Various programmes would be organised, including the Fly the Jalur Gemilang campaign and the Jelajah Jago dan Merdeka initiative."These programmes will later be compiled on the Merdeka 360 website. We encourage our media friends to help promote the platform," he said.Fahmi hoped the National Month celebrations would serve as more than just a ceremonial opening, but would ignite patriotism among Malaysians."I hope this event will not only serve as a curtain-raiser, but truly ignite the spirit of patriotism and raise the national spirit."It should help us not only remember the history of our independence and the formation of Malaysia, but also give meaning, especially to the younger generation, about the role we must play as citizens in safeguarding sovereignty, preserving unity and securing the future of our country," he said.On action against those who incorrectly display or produce inaccurate representations of the Jalur Gemilang, Fahmi said the government's initial approach would be to provide advice and guidance.He said there were existing laws related to the matter, but authorities would first engage with individuals or organisations involved to rectify the mistakes."Our first step is to advise. We will try to contact any party that has mistakenly produced content or incorrectly displayed the Jalur Gemilang and advise them to make corrections."If it is not corrected for any reason, we will then review what appropriate follow-up action should be taken. But we will begin with advice," he said.© New Straits Times Press (M) Bhd
