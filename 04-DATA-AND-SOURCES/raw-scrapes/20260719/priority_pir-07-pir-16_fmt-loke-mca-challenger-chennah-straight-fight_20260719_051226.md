The DAP secretary-general is seeking a fourth consecutive term in the mixed seat, where Malays make up 46% of voters and Chinese 44%.

Loke, who is transport minister, was accompanied by DAP chairman Gobind Singh Deo, DAP national adviser Lim Guan Eng and Amanah president Mohamad Sabu when he went to submit his nomination papers at the Dewan Majlis Daerah Jelebu this morning.

He has represented Chennah since 2013 and is seeking a fourth consecutive term as its assemblyman.

If elected, it would be his sixth term as an assemblyman, having represented Lobak previously for two terms from 2004.

Chennah is a mixed seat that falls under the Jelebu parliamentary constituency held by Umno, with Malay voters making up about 46% of the electorate, followed by the Chinese at 44%, Orang Asli at 5.9% and Indians at 2.1%.

Loke retained the seat in the 2023 state election with a 2,200-vote majority over Perikatan Nasional’s candidate when Pakatan Harapan and Barisan Nasional contested as allies.

This time around, BN is cooperating with PN while PH is going solo in all 36 seats.

The Negeri Sembilan polls will take place on Aug 1 with early voting set for July 28.
