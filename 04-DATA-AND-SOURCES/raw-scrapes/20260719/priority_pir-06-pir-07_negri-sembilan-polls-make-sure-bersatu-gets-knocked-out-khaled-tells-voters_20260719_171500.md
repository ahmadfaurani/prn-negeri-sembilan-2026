KUALA PILAH: Negri Sembilan voters should ensure Bersatu is rejected outright in the state election to pave the way for straight fights in the next general election, Umno vice-president Datuk Seri Mohamed Khaled Nordin said. He said Bersatu had been established to replace Umno and urged voters to deliver a decisive verdict against the party. "Johor has already shown the way. Negri Sembilan must confirm that decision by ensuring Bersatu is knocked out completely. "If Bersatu is eliminated here, it will make way for straight fights in the next general election and strengthen Barisan Nasional's chances of regaining federal power," he said when launching BN's election machinery and introducing Johol candidate Datuk Saiful Yazan Sulaiman here. Khaled said the electoral understanding between BN and Perikatan Nasional in Negri Sembilan should mark the beginning of greater Malay unity. He said the understanding reflected the aspirations of many Malays who were weary of prolonged political divisions and wanted to see the community reunited. "We have long yearned for Malay unity. For years, Malays have been divided and fighting among themselves. It is time for that to end. "This election is important because it can become the starting point for the reunification of the Malays, leading to a government whose core strength is the Malay community," he said. Khaled said the understanding between BN and PN had given the coalition a strategic advantage in the state election. He said the understanding had helped consolidate Malay support and placed BN in a stronger position, particularly in Malay-majority constituencies such as Johol. "The understanding is a strength for us. We are seeing a growing wave of Malay support returning to Barisan Nasional, and I believe this sentiment is not confined to Johol but is being felt across Negri Sembilan," he said. Khaled urged BN's election machinery to capitalise on the momentum by ensuring the growing support translated into votes on polling day, saying grassroots workers would play a crucial role in securing victory for the coalition. © New Straits Times Press (M) Bhd
