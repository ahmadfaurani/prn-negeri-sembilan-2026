TAMPIN: The cooperation between Barisan Nasional and Perikatan Nasional in the Tampin parliamentary constituency for the Negri Sembilan state election is aimed at avoiding clashes and does not signal a political merger between the two coalitions.

Tampin Umno division chief Datuk Mohd Isam Mohd Isa said the understanding involved the Gemas state seat, where BN was giving way to the PN candidate from Parti Wawasan Negara to face the incumbent from Bersatu as well as the Pakatan Harapan candidate.

"This understanding was established specifically for the Negri Sembilan state election.

"If this cooperation works well, the question of a merger can be discussed later, but that is not our focus right now," he said when met at the Tampin Umno building here.

Isam, who is also the Tampin member of parliament, acknowledged that some BN machinery members in Gemas were initially disappointed by the decision not to field their own candidate.

However, they eventually accepted it after being briefed on the importance of the electoral understanding.

He said all BN operations rooms in the Gemas constituency would remain open to assist the PN candidate's campaign as part of the agreement.

"We do not want any sabotage. We need to help each other because our goal is to ensure that all three state seats within the Tampin parliamentary constituency are won," he said.

Addressing concerns over potential voter confusion, he stressed that BN's struggle and principles remained unchanged despite collaborating with PN for this state election.

He said BN's relationship with its component parties, MCA and MIC, remained intact, while the pact with PN was strictly limited to electoral strategy.

The policies of BN and Pas remain different. Do not misunderstand. What we have today is merely an understanding to face the election, not a political merger. We will continue to defend BN's policies and respect the component parties that have been with us for so long, he said.

At the same time, he urged all quarters not to exploit racial and religious sentiments to win votes, particularly in Tampin, which he described as a harmonious constituency.

"Do not ruin the harmony that has long existed in Tampin by playing up religious or racial sentiments. We want this election to be conducted peacefully and maturely," he said.

Meanwhile, Isam directed all BN machinery members to practice prudent campaigning by focusing on what they could offer the people, rather than launching personal attacks or inciting provocations during the campaign period.
