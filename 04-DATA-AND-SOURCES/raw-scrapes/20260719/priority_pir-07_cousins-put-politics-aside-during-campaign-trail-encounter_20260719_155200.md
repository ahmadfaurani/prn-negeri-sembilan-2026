JELEBU: "Kirim salam kek ayah" (send my regards to your father).That was the spontaneous message from Pakatan Harapan candidate for the Klawang state seat, Datuk Bakri Sawir, to his Perikatan Nasional counterpart Danni Rais when the two cousins crossed paths during campaigning at the Kuala Klawang Sunday Market today.The two exchanged greetings and chatted briefly before Bakri asked Danni to convey his regards to his uncle, Tan Sri Dr Rais Yatim.Bakri said he had no issue greeting his cousin whenever they met during the campaign period, despite representing opposing political camps."Our family ties remain strong even though we have different political ideologies," he said.At 67, Bakri maintains an active lifestyle through regular exercise and healthy habits, including walking about 700 metres from his home to Masjid Jamek Kuala Klawang for daily Subuh prayers.He is also active in hiking and pays attention to his diet."I hope to complete the demanding two-week campaign schedule well and remain healthy, InsyaAllah," he said.On development plans for Kuala Klawang, Bakri said several initiatives would continue if he is re-elected as the assemblyman for a third term.Among them is the proposed modern rest and recreation (R&R) stop in Kampung Tembun, which was put on hold due to land status issues."There are several matters that need to be resolved before the fully equipped R&R can be built, allowing Kuala Klawang to have a proper stop for road users."The four-hectare land was found to be Malay reserve land, so the plan had to be postponed for the time being," he said.© New Straits Times Press (M) Bhd
