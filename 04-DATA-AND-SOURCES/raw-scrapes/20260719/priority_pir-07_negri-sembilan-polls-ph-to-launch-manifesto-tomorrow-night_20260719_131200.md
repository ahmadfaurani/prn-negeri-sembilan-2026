SEREMBAN: Pakatan Harapan will launch its manifesto for the 16th Negri Sembilan election tomorrow night. State election media centre secretariat head Datuk Muhammad Zaki Md Sabri, however, did not disclose details of the manifesto and urged all parties to wait for its official announcement. He said the manifesto would include various development initiatives carried out throughout the state government's administration under the Negri Sembilan PH chairman and Menteri Besar Datuk Seri Aminuddin Harun's leadership. "What I can share for now is that it will focus on the performance and achievements of the PH government under the MB's leadership, as well as efforts and plans for the state's future. "Aminuddin's strong administration will be the best factor, and the briefing sessions (on the ground) are also progressing smoothly," he told reporters. Also present was Negri Sembilan DAP secretary Cha Kee Chin. The manifesto launch is expected to be officiated by PH presidential council member and Selangor Menteri Besar Datuk Seri Amirudin Shari, who is also the joint election director for the Negri Sembilan election. Meanwhile, Zaki said the PH campaign machinery had maintained good discipline so far, carrying the key message of preserving political stability in Negri Sembilan to ensure continued progress. He said all PH candidates were focusing on their respective campaign targets and taking the opportunity to meet voters, who had given positive responses and encouragement. "Our commitment is to meet as many voters as possible to convey our candidates' commitment to becoming a bridge for resolving the rakyat's issues, rather than merely seeking votes. "So far, we are satisfied with the campaign machinery, which has ensured all campaign materials and installations comply with regulations set by the Election Commission and local authorities," he said. Zaki also said a police report had been lodged at the Seremban police headquarters by a group of members of the public against a man believed to be a member of a political party. He said the person had allegedly insulted the Yang di-Pertuan Besar of Negri Sembilan in a comment section posted on social media. "We urge all parties to maintain a positive campaign, avoid matters involving religion, royalty, and race, and refrain from insulting the Malay Rulers and traditional institutions under any circumstances. "This is a democratic contest by the rakyat that should be decided at the ballot box, not by stoking the flames of hatred and sentiment," he said. © New Straits Times Press (M) Bhd
