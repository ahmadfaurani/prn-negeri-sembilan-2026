Saw Yee Fung had questioned BN's decision to reach a pact with PAS-led PN, asking if the MCA top leadership endorsed this.

MCA Youth secretary-general Saw Yee Fung said this was conveyed by MCA secretary-general Chong Sin Woon in a text message last night, which read: “Yes, you don’t need to come for the Negeri Sembilan state election. Thank you.”

Saw criticised the MCA central leadership’s decision, questioning why the BN component could not tolerate differing voices from within, Sin Chew reported.

“We have always criticised DAP for suppressing freedom of speech (within the party).

“Could it be that now we have also changed our way of thinking and, under the leadership of certain leaders, become the very thing we once despised?

“If raising questions is now considered wrong, and expressing an opinion comes at a price, then what we stand to lose will not merely be one person’s voice, but one of the most precious values of a political party,” she was quoted as saying.

Following the confirmation of a BN-PN pact for the Negeri Sembilan polls, Saw asked the MCA top leadership whether it had endorsed the cooperation.

She was disappointed by the pact, saying she had repeatedly told voters that BN was not working with PAS during the Johor election campaign.

Saw also said she had urged Johoreans to back BN to stop the “green wave”.

BN is contesting 25 seats in the Negeri Sembilan polls while PN is vying for the remaining 11, thus ensuring any seat overlap.

Saw also said MCA’s central leadership had never held a meeting to discuss the BN-PN pact. There was also no public or private directive about the matter, she added.

“Since it was never discussed and no decision was ever made, how can I be accused of ‘failing to comply with the party central leadership’s decision’?

“And why am I not allowed to raise questions?” she said.

She added that on the night BN deputy chairman Mohamad Hasan confirmed the pact, she immediately raised the matter in the MCA central committee’s Whatsapp group chat but no one responded.

Saw acknowledged that MCA president Wee Ka Siong was currently overseas but said Chong should have been able to respond since he is leading the charge for the party in Negeri Sembilan.

“There wasn’t even a simple message to say, ‘Let’s discuss this after the president returns’,” she said.
