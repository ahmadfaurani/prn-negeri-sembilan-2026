## Full text

MELAKA: The Fire and Rescue Department has confirmed that no one was trapped after a two-storey shophouse undergoing renovation collapsed in Taman Kota Laksamana yesterday.Melaka Fire and Rescue Department director Mohd Zaidi Ma'at said the confirmation followed search operations and joint investigations with the police, the Melaka Historic City Council (MBMB), and the building owner."The fire and rescue team searched the site and found that no victims were involved in the building collapse."Joint investigations with witnesses and the building owner revealed that four workers — three Bangladeshi men in their 30s and a Rohingya man in his 40s — managed to escape unharmed," he said in a statement today.The collapse of the 30-by-80-foot shophouse also crushed two parked vehicles nearby.Zaidi said the fire department ended its operation at 11.18pm after ensuring the site was safe and posed no further danger to the public. The site has been handed over to MBMB for further action.Following the incident, MBMB issued a public notice immediately prohibiting members of the public from approaching the area, crossing the safety cordon, or entering the building.According to the notice, the restriction was enforced to safeguard public safety due to the risk of further collapse, falling debris, and other hazards that could cause injury or loss of life.The two-storey shophouse collapsed at 4.45pm, followed by a second, larger collapse at 5.23pm.© New Straits Times Press (M) Bhd
