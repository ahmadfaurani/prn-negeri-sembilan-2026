## Key Points

- **Calon Bersatu DUN Labu**, Mohamad Hanifah Abu Baker (also Ketua Pembangkang Negeri Sembilan), menyasarkan Bersatu muncul sebagai **'kingmaker'** pada PRN Negeri Sembilan ke-16
- Beliau mendakwa **kegagalan PH-BN mencapai persefahaman politik** menjadi punca pembubaran DUN sebelum tempoh matang
- Beliau juga mendakwa **PAS mengkhianati Bersatu** dalam isu kerjasama politik menjelang PRN

## Previous Election Result (PRN 2023)

- Hanifah won on **PN ticket**, defeating PH-PKR's Datuk Ismail Ahmad
- **Majority:** 1,640 votes

## Key Claims by Mohamad Hanifah

1. **PH-BN power greed:** Failure of both parties to negotiate harmoniously caused DUN dissolution, despite being in the same federal government bloc
2. **PAS betrayal:** PAS-led PN did not defend Bersatu's incumbent seat at DUN Labu, showing cracks in PN originated from PAS, not Bersatu
3. **Bersatu independence:** Bersatu now moving independently based on party principles, confident its candidates can offer people-first leadership
4. **Logo strategy:** Using Bersatu logo offers a "new offer" to Negeri Sembilan voters

## PIR-06 Assessment
**Bersatu's most senior NS figure stakes kingmaker claim.** Key intelligence:
1. **Hanifah is the Ketua Pembangkang (Opposition Leader) of NS** — Bersatu's highest-ranking elected official in the state. His framing carries weight.
2. **"Kingmaker" strategy:** Bersatu positions itself as the decisive swing force — if neither PH nor BN-PN reaches majority, Bersatu's seats determine government formation
3. **PAS "betrayal" accusation:** Direct counter to Hadi's framing. Hanifah says PAS betrayed Bersatu by not defending incumbent Labu seat for Bersatu. This is the strongest Bersatu counter-narrative to Hadi's "Bersatu straining relations" accusation.
4. **Labu (N.25) is a Tier-4 seat** — Hanifah is the incumbent (won 2023 on PN ticket, 1,640 majority). Now defends under Bersatu logo in 3-cornered fight vs PH's Ahmad Faez and BN's Siti Nur Umaira.
5. **DUN dissolution blame:** Hanifah frames the dissolution as PH-BN's failure — "ketamakan kuasa" (power greed) — they couldn't negotiate despite being in the same federal government bloc. This is a new narrative angle: the election itself is PH-BN's fault.
