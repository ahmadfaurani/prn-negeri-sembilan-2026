# PAS veep hopeful of long-term BN-PN pact

PETALING JAYA: PAS vice-president Idris Ahmad has expressed hope that the new pact between Barisan Nasional (BN) and Perikatan Nasional (PN) will go beyond the Negeri Sembilan state election.

Idris, the Perak PAS chief, said he was hopeful that the relationship between the two coalitions would be for the long term, Oriental Daily reported.

"Hopefully, the cooperation will not be limited to the Negeri Sembilan election, but will continue all the way to the end," he was quoted as saying.

Idris, a PN Supreme Council member, said the cooperation between the coalitions had been going smoothly at the grassroots level and continued to strengthen.

"In fact, this cooperation already began earlier, although it was still informal at the time," he said without disclosing when that informal cooperation started.

BN is contesting 25 of the 36 seats up for grabs in the Aug 1 polls while PN is vying for the remaining 11.

Pakatan Harapan is contesting all 36 seats, setting up a second clash with its federal ally, BN, after the Johor state election.

BN and PN's electoral pact reforges ties between Umno and PAS, the two largest Malay-Muslim parties, following the breakdown of their previous Muafakat Nasional pact.
