## Survey Overview

- **Conducted by:** Vodus Research
- **Sample:** 437 registered Negeri Sembilan voters
- **Dates:** 9–21 July 2026
- **Method:** Vodus' OMTOS online survey
- **Weighting:** Adjusted by ethnicity, age, gender per Department of Statistics Malaysia data

## Seat Projection (36 DUN — 19 needed for majority)

| Coalition | Projected Seats | Popular Vote | Net Trust Score |
|-----------|-----------------|--------------|-----------------|
| **Pakatan Harapan (PH)** | 17 | 42% | 19% |
| **Barisan Nasional (BN)** | 15 | 35% | 45% |
| **Perikatan Nasional (PN)** | 2 | 7% | 5% |
| **Undecided/Declined** | — | 14% | — |

- **2 BN-PH contests** remain too close to call
- **Neither PH nor BN has reached the 19-seat threshold** to form government
- **Hung assembly cannot be ruled out**

## Critical Dynamics

### Seat Efficiency vs. Popular Vote
- BN's support is **more efficiently distributed** across constituencies despite trailing in overall vote share
- PH leads in **8 of 10 closely contested seats**; remaining 2 undecided
- **Bersatu's presence** in 7 of 10 close contests may split Malay vote (sensitivity assumption)

### Ethnic Voter Support

| Voter Group | BN | PH | PN |
|-------------|-----|-----|-----|
| **Bumiputera** | 41% | 27% | 25% |
| **Chinese** | 20% | 59% | 9% |

- BN's Bumiputera strength provides durable statewide base
- PH's Chinese voter advantage drives stronger statewide vote share

### Trust Gap
- BN holds **significant trust advantage** (45% net trust vs. PH's 19%)
- Indicates stronger voter confidence in BN's ability to manage Negeri Sembilan

## Leadership Ratings

| Leader | Favourability Rating |
|--------|---------------------|
| Datuk Seri Aminuddin Harun (NS Menteri Besar) | 31.7% |
| Datuk Onn Hafiz Ghazi (Johor MB, earlier survey) | 58.1% |

- Gap highlights PH's challenge in translating voter support into governance confidence

## Top Voter Concerns

1. **Cost of living** — 55% (top three concerns)
2. **Jobs and wages** — 34%
3. **Housing affordability** — 26%
4. **Corruption and governance** — 24%

## Voter Certainty & Turnout

- **70%** describe current preference as completely or fairly certain
- **30%** remain open to changing their choice
- **71%** likely/very likely to vote; only **51%** in strongest "very likely" category
- Top reasons for potential non-voting: travel/outside NS, inconvenience, work commitments, living outside voting area

## Key Quote — Dr Kelvin Goh (Vodus Research Founder)

> "This remains a very close contest. BN has more clear leads, but PH holds a slight edge when closely contested seats are factored in. This means the final outcome can still go either way, and a hung assembly cannot be ruled out."

> "Ultimately, campaign performance, turnout percentage, and how parties manage the battleground of closely contested seats will determine who reaches 19 seats."

## Three Determining Factors for Final Outcome

1. BN's ability to maintain Malay voter support
2. PH maintaining Chinese & others advantage + increasing turnout
3. Both parties' ability to manage competition in closely contested seats

## PIR-07 Significance

- **17 PH + 15 BN + 2 PN + 2 undecided** = neither coalition has majority (19 needed)
- Bersatu presence in 7 of 10 close contests = potential Malay vote splitter
- PH leads 8 of 10 battleground seats but 2 remain toss-ups
- Bersatu projected to win only 2 of 24 contested seats — weak performance
- Hung assembly possibility is real

## PIR-16 Significance

- BN trust advantage (45% vs 19%) is a strategic narrative asset despite vote deficit
- Aminuddin's low favourability (31.7%) vs Johor MB (58.1%) = leadership perception challenge for PH
- 30% of voters still open to changing choice = high volatility
- Only 51% "very likely" to vote = turnout risk for both sides
