## Key Finding

Bernama survey on Day 6 of campaign period reveals voters want leaders who are not just approachable but can **deliver practical, workable solutions** to local issues.

## Voter Voices & Priorities

### Kamarulzaman Ab Aziz (64, retired teacher) — On candidate accessibility:
> "We can accept an outsider if he's likeable, easy to approach, and truly engaged with the community, because that's what counts. If a local candidate is hard to reach, then being from the area isn't really an advantage."

### Nurulainee Hamidon (37, trader/food shop owner, Linggi, Port Dickson) — On cost of living:
> "Rising prices these days are tough on buyers and also hurting small business owners. We are struggling with higher costs just to get our supplies, especially for raw ingredients. As a trader, it's really hard because our costs keep going up. But when we increase our prices, customers get angry. That's the kind of problem we have to deal with every day."

### M. Manisha (38, housewife) — On rural development:
- Urges that rural communities, particularly estate residents, not be left behind in development planning and rollout
- Essential for fair access to facilities and development opportunities

### Mohammad Najmi Aiman (22, college student, first-time voter, Paroi constituency) — On employment:
- Wants stronger focus on comprehensive employment opportunities
- Wages should truly match workers' qualifications and skills

### Lim Kian (43, private sector employee, Port Dickson) — On campaign conduct:
> "We don't want to see a culture of tearing others down among any party's machinery or supporters. Because in the end, whichever party wins will be the one governing the state, so let's keep the campaign peaceful."

- **Linggi** voter voice (Nurulainee) = cost of living pain at Aminuddin's new constituency
