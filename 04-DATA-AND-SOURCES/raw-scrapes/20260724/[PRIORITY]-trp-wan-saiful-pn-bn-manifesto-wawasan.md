## Key Development

Parti Wawasan Negara (Wawasan) vice-president Datuk Wan Saiful Wan Jan says PN and BN will soon unveil a shared manifesto for the Negeri Sembilan state election, signalling a united front for the new political alignment.

## Key Figure

- **Datuk Wan Saiful Wan Jan** — Vice-President, Parti Wawasan Negara (Wawasan)

## Core Points

1. **Joint manifesto planned:** PN and BN finalising discussions on policy commitments before announcement
2. **Concrete policies vs "empty promises":** Wan Saiful contrasts with PH — "We will present policies that will be implemented, not empty promises like Pakatan Harapan"
3. **Wawasan as bridge:** Positioning itself as party committed to bridging Malay and non-Malay political forces
4. **Negeri Sembilan as first step:** "NS is the first step in building a new political alignment for Malaysia"

## Key Quotes — Wan Saiful

> "Negeri Sembilan is the first step in building a new political alignment for Malaysia. Wawasan is offering fresh faces for voters to choose from."

> "We will announce our proposals once discussions with our partners in this new alignment are concluded. What is certain is that we will present policies that will be implemented, not empty promises like Pakatan Harapan."

> "The most important thing is that Wawasan is positioning itself as a party committed to uniting Malay and non-Malay political forces. We are prepared to serve as the bridge."

## Wawasan Seat Distribution

Wawasan contesting **4 seats** in PRN Negeri Sembilan:
- **Klawang**
- **Sikamat**
- **Mambau**
- **Gemas**

## Key Battleground — Sikamat

> An interesting seat to watch is Sikamat as Wawasan is fielding former Melaka police deputy chief **Datuk Razali Abu Samah** against Bersatu's outspoken Information Chief **Datuk Tun Faisal Ismail Aziz**.

## PIR-06 Significance

- **Early confirmation (18 Jul)** of PN-BN joint manifesto intent — later confirmed by FMT (23 Jul) as merged manifesto
- Wawasan = Bersatu splinter party, now PN component, contesting 4 seats
- Wawasan positions as "bridge" between Malay and non-Malay forces within PN
- Wawasan goes up against **Bersatu** (its former parent party) — intra-PN family split
- Sikamat battleground: Wawasan (Razali) vs Bersatu (Tun Faisal) vs PH (Nor Azman) = 3-cornered
- "New political alignment" framing = PN-BN cooperation framed as structural shift, not tactical
