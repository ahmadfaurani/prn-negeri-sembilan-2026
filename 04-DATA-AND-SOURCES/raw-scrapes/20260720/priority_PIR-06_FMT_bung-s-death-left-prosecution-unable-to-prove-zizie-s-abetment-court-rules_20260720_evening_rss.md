# Bung’s death left prosecution unable to prove Zizie’s abetment, court rules

Zizie Izette Abdul Samad faced three charges of abetting her late husband, Bung Moktar Radin, by receiving bribes on his behalf in connection with a RM2.8 million corruption case. (Bernama pic)
KUALA LUMPUR: The sessions court today ruled that the prosecution failed to prove the offence of abetment against actress Zizie Izette Abdul Samad after the charges against her husband, former Kinabatangan MP Bung Moktar Radin, were withdrawn following his death.
In delivering his oral grounds, judge Rosli Ismail said the prosecution had to prove all the essential elements of the principal offence allegedly committed by Bung before it could establish the abetment charge against Zizie.
However, he said the charges against Bung were withdrawn after his death on Dec 5 last year, making it impossible for the prosecution to establish the element of abetment.
“In this case, the charges against the principal offender were withdrawn after he passed away. On that basis, the element of abetment could not be established by the prosecution,” he said.
Rosli also found that evidence presented by both the prosecution and defence showed that Zizie had no knowledge of the investment in question.
He noted that the High Court, in September 2023, set aside the sessions court’s finding on Sept 2, 2022 that a prima facie case had been established against Bung and Zizie.
Rosli said he also accepted Zizie’s defence, as she had provided an innocent explanation for each element of the abetment charge.
“As such, the accused did not abet the offence through conspiracy or common design, and the introduction fee was not a bribe,” he said.
On the RM2.8 million allegedly received as gratification, Rosli said the defence had consistently maintained that the payment was an introduction fee rather than a bribe. This was consistent with the testimony of prosecution witnesses Madhi Abdul Hamid and Norhaili Ahmad Mokhtar.
However, the judge declined to make any definitive finding on whether the RM2.8 million constituted gratification, saying the issue had been extensively argued at the prima facie stage and could be determined if the prosecution pursued an appeal.
Speaking to reporters outside the court, Zizie said she wished her late husband had been by her side to witness the verdict.
“Alhamdulillah, I am deeply grateful for today’s decision. When the judgment was delivered, I imagined he was standing next to me,” she said.
“It has been seven years, and finally justice has prevailed. I have a huge responsibility as I have five children to care for, and I am now a single mother. Please allow me to move on with my life.”
The case stemmed from Felcra Bhd’s RM150 million investment in Public Mutual unit trust funds in 2015.
The prosecution alleged that Bung, who was then Felcra’s non-executive chairman, corruptly received RM2.8 million through Zizie as an inducement to help secure approval for the investment. Zizie was accused of abetting the offence.
During the trial, the prosecution called 30 witnesses, including former second finance minister Ahmad Husni Hanadzlah, former Felcra Investments general manager Adnan Yusof, Felcra board member Habibah Suleiman, and Public Mutual investment agents Madhi Abdul Hamid and Norhaili Ahmad Mokhtar.
Madhi testified that the money paid to Zizie comprised agent’s fees or introduction fees, insisting that Bung had never requested any payment, there was no discussion that the money was a bribe, and he had never intended the payment for Bung.
Norhaili similarly testified that while payments were made to Zizie, Bung never requested gratification, there was no agreement to pay him, and the money was not a reward for securing Felcra’s approval of the RM150 million investment.
The Court of Appeal in November 2024 allowed the prosecution’s appeal to reinstate the charges and ordered both accused to enter their defence, paving the way for today’s verdict.
Then sessions court judge Rozina Ayob ruled that the prosecution had established a prima facie case. Rosli took over the case when the defence began its case last year.
