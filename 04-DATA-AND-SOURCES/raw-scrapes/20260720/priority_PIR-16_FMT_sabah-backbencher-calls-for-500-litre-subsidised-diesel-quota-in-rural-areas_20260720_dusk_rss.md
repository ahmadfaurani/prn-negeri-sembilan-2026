# Sabah backbencher calls for 500-litre subsidised diesel quota in rural areas

PETALING JAYA: The monthly quota for subsidised diesel in Sabah’s rural areas should be increased from 200 litres to 500 litres to better meet the needs of long-distance travel, particularly in the state’s interior, the Sabah state assembly heard today.
Masiung Banah (GRS-Kuamut) said the current 200-litre quota is insufficient for rural areas such as Kuamut, where residents and elected representatives must travel for hours to reach urban centres when attending to various matters.
“The diesel subsidy, which has been capped at 200 litres, is not enough for my constituency. A round trip from Kuamut to Kota Kinabalu takes six to seven hours.
“I don’t have the exact distance in kilometres, but what is certain is that 200 litres would already be used up on a single return journey between Kuamut and Kota Kinabalu,” Bernama quoted him as saying.
Masiung, who chairs the Sabah Backbenchers Club, said this during the winding-up of the debate on the Supplementary Supply Bill 2026.
He said the implementation of the targeted diesel subsidy should take into account the different circumstances between urban and rural areas, particularly in terms of travel distance, terrain and area size.
While acknowledging Putrajaya’s challenges in restructuring fuel subsidies, he said the needs of rural communities must not be overlooked.
Implemented nationwide from July 1, the BUDI Diesel programme gives eligible private diesel vehicle owners a basic subsidised quota of 200 litres per month at RM2.10 per litre.
Eligible owners of sport utility vehicles and pickup trucks may also apply for an additional 100 litres per month.
