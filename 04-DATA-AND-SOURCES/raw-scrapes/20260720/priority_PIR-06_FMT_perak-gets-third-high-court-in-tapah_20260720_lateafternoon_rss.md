# Perak gets third High Court in Tapah

PETALING JAYA: Perak has become the only state in Peninsular Malaysia to have High Courts in three major towns following the commencement of operations of the High Court in Tapah.
The new High Court, which began operations last Friday, is the state’s third High Court after Ipoh and Taiping. Judicial commissioner Zaman Nor has been appointed to preside over the new court.
The Tapah court complex at Jalan Bidor Lama also houses the magistrates’ and sessions courts.
Chief Judge of Malaya Hashim Hamzah said the establishment of the Tapah High Court marked another milestone in the country’s judicial history and formed part of the judiciary’s efforts to strengthen the administration of justice and improve public access to judicial services.
He said the new High Court would hear and determine civil and criminal cases from five districts: Hilir Perak, Bagan Datuk, Batang Padang, Muallim and Kampar.
Existing cases filed in the Ipoh High Court that fall within the Tapah High Court’s territorial jurisdiction will be transferred to the new court.
The Perak Bar meanwhile advised members to take note of the changes when filing new proceedings, managing existing cases and advising clients on the appropriate forum and venue for legal proceedings.
