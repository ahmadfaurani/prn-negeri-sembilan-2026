Secretary of state Marco Rubio said the US remains committed to pursuing diplomacy with Iran. (AFP pic)
WASHINGTON: US secretary of state Marco Rubio said Sunday that Iran is using the Strait of Hormuz as leverage in the ongoing Middle East conflict, adding that other countries needed to step up pressure to protect global shipping.
“It’s clear that Iran, at least some people in Iran, want to control the straits and hold that as leverage against the world,” Rubio told reporters ahead of traveling to the Philippines for the meeting of Asean foreign ministers.
“The United States will do and continue to do what it needs to, to protect global shipping, but other countries need to begin to step up and provide, whether it’s hardware or finances, to help carry that burden,” he continued.
When asked if there was still a diplomatic off-ramp to the escalating conflict, Rubio said: “I think we’re always open to diplomacy. It has to be real. It has to be a deal that they’re willing to live by.”
A fragile truce between Iran and the US broke down after reported attacks by Iran on ships in the Strait of Hormuz, the critical international waterway through which a fifth of the global oil and gas supply transited prior to the war.
“They can’t have an MOU that’s alive if they’re violating the terms of it,” Rubio said.
“What I will say is that the United States always remains open to a diplomatic solution. We’ve tried multiple times with Iran, and we’ll continue to try.”
