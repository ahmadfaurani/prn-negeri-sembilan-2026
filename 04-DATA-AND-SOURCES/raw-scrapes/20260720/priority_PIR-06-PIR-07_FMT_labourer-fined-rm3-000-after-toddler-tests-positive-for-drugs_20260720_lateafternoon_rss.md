PETALING JAYA: A labourer was fined RM3,000 by the Pasir Mas sessions court in Kelantan after pleading guilty to exposing his 20-month-old daughter to drugs, causing her to test positive for benzodiazepines and methamphetamine.
Shahrul Hisham Lailatulazhma, 27, pleaded guilty before judge Zulkpli Abdullah, who ordered him to serve three months in prison if he fails to pay the fine.
The court also imposed a RM2,000 good behaviour bond for a year and ordered Shahrul to perform 36 hours of community service, Berita Harian reported.
Shahrul was charged with exposing his daughter to dangerous drugs in a manner likely to cause her physical harm at Taman Rantau, Rantau Panjang, at 10.30pm on April 4.
The charge under Section 31(1)(a) of the Child Act 2001 carries a maximum fine of RM20,000, up to 10 years’ jail, or both upon conviction.
Deputy public prosecutor Siti Aisyha Nai’lah Harizan urged the court to impose a deterrent sentence, citing the victim’s young age.
She added that the child’s father had a duty to protect the toddler but instead exposed her to drugs.
Shahrul, who was unrepresented, appealed for a minimal sentence, saying he was remorseful and needed to support his family as his wife was unemployed.
