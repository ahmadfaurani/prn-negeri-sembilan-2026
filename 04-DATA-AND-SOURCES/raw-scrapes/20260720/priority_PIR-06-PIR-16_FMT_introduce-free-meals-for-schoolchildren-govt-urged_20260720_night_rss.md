# Introduce free meals for schoolchildren, govt urged

PETALING JAYA: A former MP has called on Putrajaya to consider providing free meals to schoolchildren nationwide, expanding on an initiative by the Selangor government which starts next month.
DAP’s Charles Santiago said the federal government should support the state government’s initiative to provide free breakfast to 120,000 schoolchildren from August, and help expand it to other states.
“At a time when families are struggling with the rising cost of living, no child should have to learn on an empty stomach,” the former Klang MP said in a statement.
The Selangor government’s programme, reported to be part of a RM140 million economic relief package, will see 120,000 pupils in 965 schools across the state receive free breakfast two to three times a week.
Students already receiving meals under the federal Supplementary Food Programme (RMT) scheme will not be included. The RMT only targets children from lower-income households.
Santiago said these food programmes for students could create jobs and support local farmers as well as small food vendors if ingredients were sourced locally.
He cited Brazil, where more than 30% of the school meals budget is channelled to family farms.
He also suggested that Putrajaya revisit a previous proposal to provide free breakfast to about 2.7 million pupils in government and government-aided primary schools nationwide.
He was referring to a proposed nationwide free breakfast programme announced in 2019 that was supposed to benefit 2.7 million primary school pupils.
Initially expected to cost the government between RM800 million and RM1.67 billion, it was later scaled back amid
concerns over cost
and whether children from wealthier families should qualify.
