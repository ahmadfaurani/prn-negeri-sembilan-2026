SHAH ALAM: The High Court here today maintained a 10-year jail sentence against a former company executive for statutory rape and extortion against a 14-year-old teenager.
Justice Hazlina Hussain affirmed the 10-year jail sentence against Ooi Vin Shen, 29, for statutory rape, and one-year jail sentence for extortion.
Ooi, who is serving both sentences concurrently, is also to be whipped twice.
Hazlina said the sessions court did not make any error in law when convicting Ooi last year on charges under Sections 376(1) and 384 of the Penal Code.
“The victim was a credible witness and gave detailed evidence during the trial,” she said.
“The sessions court was right to accept her evidence, along with supporting evidence from two doctors who examined her.”
She also pointed out that the victim became suicidal when Ooi blackmailed her for RM2,300 after the rape.
According to the facts of the case, Ooi met the girl through a dating app in 2023 and raped her after going out with her on a few dates.
He also took nude photographs of the victim and blackmailed her by threatening to circulate them online if she did not pay him.
He forced her to provide sexual services to other men to earn money for the payment.
Lawyer Ho Cheng En represented Ooi, while deputy public prosecutor Tengku Najiha Tengku Zahari appeared for the prosecution.
