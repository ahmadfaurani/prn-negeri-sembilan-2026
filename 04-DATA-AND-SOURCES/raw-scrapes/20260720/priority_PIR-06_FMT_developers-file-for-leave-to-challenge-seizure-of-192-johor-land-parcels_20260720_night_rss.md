# Developers file for leave to challenge seizure of 192 Johor land parcels

KUALA LUMPUR: Four property development companies have filed an application for leave to commence judicial review proceedings to challenge the seizure of 192 parcels of land in Johor under the Anti-Money Laundering, Anti-Terrorism Financing and Proceeds of Unlawful Activities Act 2001 (Amla).
The companies, subsidiaries of United Malayan Land Bhd (UMLand), must first obtain the court’s leave before substantive merits of their application can be heard.
The applicants are Tentu Teguh Sdn Bhd, Viridea Lakeside Sdn Bhd, Exquisite Mode Sdn Bhd and UMLand Medini Lakeside Development Sdn Bhd.
They named Attorney-General Dusuki Mokhtar, deputy public prosecutor Wan Ahmad Hakimi Wan Ahmad Jaafar, Inspector-General of Police Khalid Ismail, Bukit Aman’s Amla investigation unit chief Fazlisyam Abd Majid, investigating officer Rozimi Mustapha and the government as respondents.
In the leave application filed on July 10 and sighted by FMT, the companies sought a certiorari order to quash seizure orders dated May 25, 2026, issued under Section 51(1) of Amla.
The orders concern 32 land parcels owned by Tentu Teguh, three by Viridea Lakeside, 156 by Exquisite Mode and one by UMLand Medini Lakeside Development.
They also sought a mandamus order compelling the respondents to take all necessary steps to give effect to any order quashing the seizure orders, including cancelling the orders and removing the registrar’s caveats registered against the affected properties.
The application states that the seizure orders followed earlier freezing orders issued on Feb 27, 2026, under Section 44 of Amla.
The companies are also seeking costs and any other relief the High Court considered appropriate.
UMLand general manager of group corporate legal, Jenni Teh, affirmed an affidavit in support of the application, stating that the companies were seeking leave for a judicial review to challenge the legality of the seizure orders on the grounds that the properties seized were unrelated to the subject matter of the Amla investigation.
The affidavit claimed that the police and prosecution improperly seized properties belonging to the four UMLand subsidiaries, despite the properties allegedly having no connection to a corporate demerger dispute under investigation.
It further claimed that the seizure orders had caused serious commercial prejudice to the companies.
Justice Azizan Arshad will hear the leave application on Aug 4.
