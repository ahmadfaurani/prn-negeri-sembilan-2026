# AGC orders inquest into fatal Durian Tunggal police shooting

PETALING JAYA: The Attorney-General’s Chambers (AGC) has ordered an inquest into the deaths of three men who were shot by police in Durian Tunggal, Melaka, in November last year.
The AGC said the purpose of the inquest is to determine the cause and circumstances of the deaths and to examine the facts and circumstances that led to them, including establishing whether any criminal elements were involved.
“After considering the entirety of the evidence and the findings of the investigation, the AGC has decided that an inquest be held under Section 339(1) of the Criminal Procedure Code,” it said in a statement.
It said the decision to hold an inquest reflects the AGC’s commitment to ensuring that all relevant facts and circumstances are examined thoroughly, transparently and independently, in accordance with the law.
Home minister Saifuddin Nasution Ismail earlier this month said the investigation papers into the incident were resubmitted to the AGC on four occasions – Dec 16, 2025; Dec 29, 2025; April 14, this year and most recently on May 25.
In May, federal criminal investigation department director M Kumar said the police received further instructions each time the probe papers were submitted to the AGC.
He added that statements were recorded from a total of 63 witnesses.
In the incident, which occurred at about 4.30am on Nov 24 last year, three men aged between 24 and 29 were shot dead after one of them allegedly attacked a police officer with a parang.
Investigations were opened under Section 307 of the Penal Code for attempted murder. However, in December, the AGC directed that the case be reclassified as murder.
On July 7, DAP national chairman Gobind Singh Deo
called on
Attorney-General Dusuki Mokhtar to explain the delay in taking action against those responsible for the fatal police shooting of the three men.
