# Russian opposition figure Nadezhdin withdraws from election

MOSCOW: Russian anti-war politician Boris Nadezhdin on Sunday said he will not be running in September’s legislative elections, saying he has been “silenced” and “pushed out of politics”.
Nadezhdin — one of the few politicians in Russia openly criticising president Vladimir Putin and Moscow’s Ukraine offensive — had recently been convicted for displaying “extremist symbols”.
“My ability to legally engage in opposition politics in Russia has been exhausted,” he wrote on Telegram.
“I’m not prepared to put at risk those who support me and took part in the election campaign”
The 63-year-old’s campaign had been severely compromised since he was declared a “foreign agent” last week, followed by his sentencing on Friday for “displaying extremist symbols”, in this case posting a video in 2023 that briefly showed deceased dissident Alexei Navalny.
Navalny and his organisations had been designated as “extremist” prior to his death in prison in 2024. Since then, the authorities have regularly accused individuals of promoting “extremism” for sharing his comments or even his photographs.
Since the full-scale offensive against Ukraine in February 2022, the Russian authorities have significantly intensified their crackdown, imprisoning hundreds of their critics.
Almost all opponents are now either in prison, dead or in exile. Nadezhdin was one of the few people in Russia to publicly denounce Putin and the offensive in Ukraine without being imprisoned.
