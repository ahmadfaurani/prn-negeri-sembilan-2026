PETALING JAYA: Barisan Nasional (BN) has chosen to cooperate with Perikatan Nasional (PN) in the Negeri Sembilan state election to send Pakatan Harapan (PH) a signal, says former Umno Youth chief Khairy Jamaluddin.
Speaking to reporters after attending a Subang Umno meeting last night, Khairy said some Umno members felt the federal government was not paying enough attention to Malay sensitivities, and had also failed to fulfil various promises it had made.
In a Sinar Harian report, Khairy also claimed that PH often used Umno’s presence in the unity government as an excuse for failing to implement its reform agenda.
“It is as if they are blaming Umno for their own failure to push through reforms and fulfil their promises,” he said.
However, Khairy said that BN did not need to leave the unity government or push for an early general election.
“We can wait until the general election. We are only taking this opportunity to send a signal to PH not to take Umno and BN for granted,” he said.
Ties between PH and BN soured after BN decided to go solo in the July 11 state election in Johor. PH had wanted both coalitions to contest as allies, similar to the pact they had for the six state elections in 2023.
The relationship appeared to deteriorate further with BN’s decision to form an alliance with PN in the Aug 1 Negeri Sembilan election.
In the Negeri Sembilan election, PH is contesting all 36 seats on its own, while BN and PN have reached an electoral understanding under which BN is contesting 25 seats and PN 11.
In the recently concluded Johor election, PAS had urged its members and supporters to back BN candidates in seats where PN did not contest.
