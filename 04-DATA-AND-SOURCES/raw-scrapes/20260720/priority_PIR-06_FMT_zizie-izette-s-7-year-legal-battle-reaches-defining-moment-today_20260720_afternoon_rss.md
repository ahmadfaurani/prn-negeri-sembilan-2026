# Zizie Izette’s 7-year legal battle reaches defining moment today

KUALA LUMPUR: After seven years of legal battles, appeals and unexpected twists, former actress Zizie Izette Abdul Samad will finally learn today whether she will be convicted or acquitted of abetting her late husband, former Kinabatangan MP Bung Moktar Radin, in a RM2.8 million corruption case.
The verdict will mark a significant moment in one of the country’s most closely watched corruption trials involving a politician and his spouse.
The case began in 2019 and has gone through a number of stages in court, including a High Court acquittal that was later overturned on appeal.
The couple was charged in May 2019 over allegations that Bung, who was then Felcra Bhd’s non-executive chairman, had received RM2.8 million in bribes from Public Mutual investment agents as an inducement to help secure approval for Felcra’s RM150 million investment in unit trust funds in 2015.
Zizie faces three charges of abetting her husband by receiving the gratification on his behalf.
Bung faced three corruption charges – two involving payments allegedly made by Public Mutual agent Madhi Abdul Hamid, and another involving agent Norhaili Ahmad Mokhtar.
The trial began in January 2020, with the prosecution calling 30 witnesses, including Felcra officials, Malaysian Anti-Corruption Commission investigators, and the two investment agents who allegedly made the payments.
The testimonies of Madhi and Norhaili became a key focus of the trial. Both admitted making payments to Zizie, but denied that the money was meant as bribes or that Bung had demanded any payment from them.
The prosecution argued that the circumstances surrounding the payments and related transactions showed that the money was gratification intended for Bung.
In September 2022, the sessions court found that the prosecution had established a prima facie case and ordered both accused to enter their defence.
However, the case took a dramatic turn in September 2023 when the High Court acquitted them after ruling that the sessions court had failed to properly evaluate key evidence.
The prosecution appealed, and in November 2024, the Court of Appeal overturned the High Court’s decision and restored the order requiring both accused to enter their defence.
During the defence stage, Bung denied knowing about the alleged payments and maintained that he had never solicited or received any gratification.
Zizie also denied wrongdoing, insisting that the money she received was not connected to any corrupt arrangement involving her husband.
The proceedings took another turn following Bung’s death in December 2025. The prosecution discontinued the case against him, resulting in his acquittal, while the charge against Zizie continued as she faced her own alleged role in the offences.
Today, the trial court will decide whether the prosecution has proven beyond reasonable doubt that Zizie knowingly assisted Bung in committing the alleged offences, or whether her defence has raised sufficient doubt to warrant an acquittal.
While the verdict will conclude the trial proceedings that began seven years ago, it may not be the final chapter, as either side may still appeal if dissatisfied with the decision.
