# Supermarket at Gurney Drive mall shut over hygiene breach

GEORGE TOWN: The Penang health department has temporarily shut down a well-known supermarket at a Gurney Drive mall over hygiene violations uncovered during a routine inspection.
State health committee chairman Daniel Gooi said the inspection was part of periodic enforcement efforts to ensure food premises in the state comply with food safety and cleanliness standards.
“This was not a special operation but routine enforcement carried out from time to time. Action will be taken against premises that fail to meet hygiene requirements,” he told reporters at a press conference at Komtar today.
The supermarket was issued a closure notice yesterday over cleanliness issues and will remain closed until Aug 2.
Gooi maintained that the action was not meant to penalise operators but to protect public health and ensure businesses operate in a safe and hygienic environment.
“We have also seen several food poisoning cases this year and want to prevent similar incidents from recurring,” he said, adding that he would seek further details from the state health department regarding the action taken against the supermarket.
Separately, he said cases of Covid-19; hand, foot and mouth disease (HFMD); and dengue in Penang remained under control, with all three on a downward trend.
As of the 26th epidemiological week for 2026, Covid-19 cases dropped by 59.7% compared with the same period last year, while HFMD cases were down by 86.5%.
He said dengue cases were also declining but did not disclose the figures. Gooi nonetheless advised the public to remain vigilant, as the number of cases nationally was rising.
