KUALA PILAH: Umno vice-president Khaled Nordin has urged voters in Negeri Sembilan to ensure that Bersatu suffers the same wipeout it did in Johor, saying the party will only split the votes that would otherwise go to Barisan Nasional (BN) and Perikatan Nasional (PN).
He said Bersatu, which he claimed was set up to replace Umno, must be completely rejected so it would have no footing in the 16th general election.
“Many (Bersatu candidates) lost their deposits (in Johor). I hope that Negeri Sembilan will ensure that Bersatu gets no support in the state.
“Make sure Bersatu is completely knocked out in Negeri Sembilan,” he said at the launch of BN’s election machinery in Johol here last night.
Khaled said the cooperation between BN and PN in Negeri Sembilan would pave the way for a stronger state administration if BN wins the majority of the 25 seats it is contesting, with support from the 11 seats being contested by PN.
“In Johor, we were contesting against many parties. We ran against PAS, Bersatu and Pakatan Harapan (PH).
“But in Negeri Sembilan, we have an advantage that we did not have in Johor: an understanding between BN and PN,” he said.
Negeri Sembilan will head to the polls on Aug 1. BN and PN have an electoral understanding under which BN will contest 25 seats and PN 11, with no overlap. Bersatu is contesting 24 seats under its own banner, while PH is contesting all 36 seats.
