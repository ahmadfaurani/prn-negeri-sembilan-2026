# Formula BN-PN Tawan Semua Negeri di Semenanjung

**Source:** Utusan Malaysia | **Date:** 22 Julai 2026 | **Author:** Badrul Hafizan Mat Isa
**URL:** https://www.utusan.com.my/nasional/2026/07/formula-bn-pn-tawan-semua-negeri-di-semenanjung/
**PIR Tags:** PIR-06 (Coalition Operational Arrangement)

---

## Key Figures
- **Datuk Seri Tuan Ibrahim Tuan Man** — Timbalan Presiden PAS
- **Ahmad Fadhli Shaari** — Ketua Penerangan PAS Pusat

## Core Strategy: BN-PN Political Cooperation

The political understanding between **Barisan Nasional (BN)** and **Perikatan Nasional (PN)** could enable both coalitions to jointly capture nearly all states in Peninsular Malaysia, based on vote calculations from previous elections.

### Key Quote — Tuan Ibrahim Tuan Man:
> "Menerusi kerjasama politik yang dapat dijalinkan ini, Insya-Allah kita akan boleh menang sebahagian besar kerusi-kerusi yang dimenangi oleh Pakatan Harapan (PH)."

> "Malah di Pulau Pinang juga, kalau kita lihat memang PH agak lebih ke depan sikit tetapi ada pengundi-pengundi Cina yang menolak DAP terutamanya kerana mereka kecewa dengan keadaan ekonomi, jadi mereka ini akan beri undi kepada BN dan PN."

## Key Insights

### 1. Penang — Chinese Voter Shift
- PH currently leads in Penang
- Some **Chinese voters are rejecting DAP** due to **economic disappointment**
- These voters may shift support to BN and PN

### 2. Reciprocal Action — Selangor vs. Perak/Pahang
- PKR is pressuring UMNO leaders in Selangor to relinquish state government positions
- Tuan Ibrahim argues **the same should apply to PH leaders in Perak and Pahang**
- In Pahang and Perak, PH could exit the state government

### 3. Accelerated General Election
> "Jika senario ini berlaku, tekanan-tekanan ini, berkemungkinan besar akan makin cepat dilaksanakan pilihan raya umum."

## Ahmad Fadhli Shaari's Assessment
> "Kalaupun mereka (PH) boleh bertahan, hanya di Pulau Pinang sahaja tetapi Insya-Allah seluruh Semenanjung Malaysia ini akan berjaya kita tawan."

### Proposed Election Sequence:
1. **Negeri Sembilan** (current focus)
2. **Melaka**
3. **Sarawak**
4. Potential simultaneous dissolution of: Kelantan, Terengganu, Kedah, Melaka
5. Possible **general election** alongside

## Context
- Statement made at **Ceramah PN di UDM Jiboi, Ampangan, Seremban**
