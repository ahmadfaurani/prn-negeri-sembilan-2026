PORT DICKSON: Ahmad Zahid Hamidi’s challenge to Anwar Ibrahim to sack eight Barisan Nasional Cabinet ministers if the prime minister does not like them is uncalled for, says a Pakatan Harapan leader. Aminuddin Harun, the Negeri Sembilan PH chairman, said the deputy prime minister’s statement gave the impression that he was challenging Anwar’s authority. “The remark, if indeed true, should not have come from a good friend of the prime minister,” he said while campaigning in Tanjung Agas, Linggi. Earlier today, Zahid, who is the BN chairman, issued the challenge in response to Anwar’s call for those who attacked their government colleagues while campaigning during state elections to step down. Zahid, however, said he saw no reason for any of BN’s eight ministers to resign, “because the prime minister is the one who appointed ministers and deputy ministers”. “If he doesn’t like us, just sack us. But he wouldn’t have the heart to sack us. All of us work very hard,” Zahid was reported as saying. Aminuddin, who is contesting in Linggi, said Anwar’s remarks were merely a reminder to ensure that the ties in the unity government would not be affected by those campaigning in the Negeri Sembilan state election. On a separate matter, Aminuddin also hit out at BN over the alleged use of government assets when campaigning for the state polls. He said BN had criticised such practices when it was in the opposition. “Yet today, some of them (BN representatives) in government are using government assets.” Zahid had earlier shrugged off the accusations, saying others had done the same. He added that while he did not want to point fingers at anyone, he had “proof”. Zahid had also questioned the alleged double standards of those who took issue with the matter, saying the critics only objected to the use of government assets when it suited them.
