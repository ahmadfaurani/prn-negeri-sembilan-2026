## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This document integrates 74 field audit records from the "Laporan Terperinci Isu DUN N9" field audit exercise into the 18-PIR Negeri Sembilan PRN 2026 intelligence framework. The audit covers 17 of 36 DUN constituencies across 5 parliamentary seats (P.126 Jelebu, P.127 Jempol, P.128 Seremban, P.130 Rasah, P.131 Rembau, P.133 Tampin), with 48 substantive auditor notes containing political intelligence. The data was collected during the immediate post-nomination period (18–20 July), representing the earliest systematic ground-level voter sentiment capture of the campaign.

**Key Findings:**
- 42 records classified "Safe," 14 "Marginal," 9 "Hard to Win," 9 "Potential Win" — broadly consistent with PH's expected position but revealing pockets of significant vulnerability
- STR cash aid reduction (RM100→RM50) is a **corroborated cross-constituency pattern** affecting voter sentiment in multiple DUNs
- Racial narrative weaponization ("sentimen perkauman," "DAP kontrol gov") is **corroborated across at least 5 DUNs** — this is the most persistent opposition narrative at grassroots level
- PKR machinery friction with candidates is **corroborated** in at least 2 DUNs (Sungai Lui, Ampangan) — candidate-cabang conflicts pose escalation risk
- BN FELDA machinery remains a **structural advantage** in rural/FELDA-settlement DUNs
- Youth political disengagement is **corroborated** — millennials and Gen Alpha described as "tidak celik politik"
- Machinery visibility gaps reported in multiple DUNs — "kekurangan kemeriahan" and funding constraints for BGU operations

**Audit Coverage Gaps:** 8 DUNs listed in the audit summary table have no detailed records in the body (N.04 Klawang, N.05 Sungai Lui additional, N.08, N.11, N.15, N.16, N.17, N.18, N.19, N.20, N.23, N.24, N.25, N.29, N.30, N.31, N.32, N.33 — representing significant intelligence blind spots, particularly for T1 battleground seats like N.25 Paroi and N.32 Linggi).

### NS-04: DUN-Level Electoral Risk

**Priority Intelligence Question:**
Which DUN constituencies are most likely to change political control, and what factors will determine the result?

**Assessment:**

The field audit provides DUN-by-DUN classification across 17 constituencies, cross-referenced below with 2023 baseline results. The audit data reveals a more granular picture than the daily brief's sentiment tracking: while the campaign trail shows PH consolidating its position (Aminuddin confirmed as MB, SARA increase announced), the ground-level audit reveals persistent pockets of vulnerability driven by STR aid reduction narratives, racial sentiment, and BN FELDA machinery strength.

**Key DUN-level findings from audit data:**

1. **N.02 Pertang (avg 69.8, S:5 H:1):** 2023 BN incumbent Jalaluddin won with 32.91% margin. Audit shows 5 Safe / 1 Hard to Win. The "Hard to Win" record relates to BGU coordination failures with Melaka angkat state, not voter sentiment per se. Daily brief confirms Jalaluddin actively campaigning with Hamzah Zainudin (PN deputy chairman) — BN-PN machinery sharing is operationally active here. PH candidate Mohd Umry faces a 3-cornered contest. Classification: **Competitive** — BN incumbent advantage but multi-cornered contest creates vote-splitting opportunity.

2. **N.03 Sungai Lui (avg 64.5, S:1 M:2):** PKR machinery friction reported — "jentera tidak support calon sangat sebab ada isu calon terima dana dari BN." Candidate is a former schoolteacher with local acceptance. FELDA areas remain BN strongholds. Classification: **Competitive** — candidate acceptance issue creates internal risk.

3. **N.10 Nilai (avg 42.9, M:1 H:2):** Lowest average score among all DUNs with detailed records. "Sentimen DAP" explicitly cited as issue. One respondent shifted from PH to BN. Classification: **Vulnerable** — DAP narrative weaponization and pendatang asing concerns.

4. **N.21 Bukit Kepayang (avg 49.8, PW:1 H:2):** Youth disengagement prominently reported. One respondent (Haji Azim) will vote PH regardless of candidate. Two "Hard to Win" records relate to youth political apathy. Classification: **Vulnerable** — turnout risk among youth demographics.

5. **N.26 Chembong (avg 48.3, M:1):** Single record from UMNO youth chief respondent. Classification: **Competitive** — insufficient data for full assessment.

6. **N.27 Rantau (avg 51.3, M:2):** Both records Marginal. Diaspora voter (postal) expresses frustration with M40 tax burden vs B40 benefits. Classification: **Competitive**.

7. **N.28 Kota (avg 68.7, S:10 PW:2 M:3 H:3):** Largest sample (18 records). Mixed picture — strong PH areas (Chuah) alongside hard BN areas. "Cina Tua dia basuh otak untuk sokong MCA BN" and BN-only access to Orang Asli settlements reported. One "Hard to Win" record describes Indian community anger at PH incumbent. Classification: **Competitive** — polarized with significant BN pockets.

8. **N.13 Sikamat (avg 77.7, S:5 PW:1 M:1):** Daily brief confirms Anwar confirmed Aminuddin as MB here — major positive development. One Marginal record (normadiah) is a "penyokong tegar BN" with racial sentiment. Classification: **Favourable** — MB confirmation strengthens position.

9. **N.14 Ampangan (avg 78.9, S:8 PW:3 M:2):** 13 records — second-largest sample. Candidate-cabang conflict reported ("calon ada masaalah dgn ketua cabang, masa pengumuman nama calon kena boo dgn ahli"). Also: "sentimen dap kontrol gov kuat, isu sara 100." Daily brief: Anwar named Ampangan at Sikamat dinner. Classification: **Favourable** but with internal machinery friction risk.

### NS-05: Daerah Mengundi and Locality Prioritisation

**Priority Intelligence Question:**
Which Daerah Mengundi and localities offer the greatest opportunity for vote consolidation, persuasion or turnout improvement?

**Assessment:**

The audit data provides locality classification for 50 of 74 records (24 unclassified). The Separa Bandar (26) vs Luar Bandar (20) split reveals distinct issue profiles and intervention requirements.

**Separa Bandar findings (26 records):**
- Higher overall audit scores (averaging 70+) — PH base stronger in semi-urban areas
- Dominant issues: cost of living, employment, internet, housing
- Key vulnerability: "sentimen dap kontrol gov" reported in Separa Bandar localities (N.14 Ampangan, N.36 Repah)
- STR aid reduction narrative present but less acute than in Luar Bandar

**Luar Bandar findings (20 records):**
- Lower overall scores — BN traditional strength
- Dominant issues: infrastructure (jalan raya, lampu jalan), flood, security (haiwan liar), welfare
- STR aid reduction more acutely felt — direct cash aid is more significant for B40 rural households
- BN FELDA machinery advantage is structural — "org felda lebih berat utk mengundi UMNO sbb igt bantuan semua dtg dari UMNO"
- Ketua kampung play decisive role — "ketua kampung main peranan besar (BN)"

**Specific localities requiring intervention:**

1. **FELDA settlements (N.02 Pertang, N.03 Sungai Lui, N.36 Repah):** BN machinery dominance. Ketua kampung aligned to BN. PH aid delivered but credit claimed by BN. [CORROBORATED REPORTING — multiple auditors across 3 DUNs]

2. **Orang Asli settlements — Kg Orang Asli Air Sungai Jernih (N.35 Gemencheh):** BN-only access. "Ade 1 tempat hanya BN sahaja boleh masuk. kampung org asli air sungai jernih." [SINGLE-SOURCE REPORTING — one auditor, one KOL, but consistent with known BN-Orang Asli access patterns]

3. **Kampung Pulau Dalam, Kota (N.28 Kota):** BN stronghold with racial sentiment. "Kuat pada BN... kuat bermain sentimen cina dan melayu." [SINGLE-SOURCE REPORTING]

4. **Sikamat Rohingya area (N.13 Sikamat):** Rohingya influx cited as major local concern — "MASALAH PALING BESAR LAMBAKKAN ROHINGYA." Opens schools and food stalls. [CORROBORATED REPORTING — 2 auditors in same DUN]

5. **Senaling-Inas road (N.14 Ampangan/Johol):** Lampu jalan deficiency cited by Amanah machinery. "Jentera sekitar 5-6 ke setiap tempat berkempen." [SINGLE-SOURCE REPORTING]

6. **Bandar Sunggala, Linggi (N.28 Kota):** PBT enforcement issues; flood; unlicensed renovations. [SINGLE-SOURCE REPORTING]

> "kampung org asli air sungai jernih" — "Ade 1 tempat hanya BN sahaja boleh masuk."
>
> *Analytical note: BN-exclusive access to Orang Asli settlements represents a structural intelligence blind spot. PH has no ground presence in these localities. This is consistent with historical BN-Orang Asli community patronage networks across Peninsular Malaysia.*

> "org UMNO yg jaga, org felda lebih berat utk mengundi UMNO sbb igt bantuan semua dtg dari UMNO. jadi bila nak PRN @ PRU, terutamanya saluran 6 lebih kenal UMNO@BN berbanding PH."
>
> *Analytical note: BN FELDA machinery exploits the perception that all government aid flows through UMNO channels. Even when PH government delivers aid, ketua kampung from BN claim credit. This is a structural advantage that requires sustained counter-messaging — not just campaign-period intervention.*

> "Semasa BN-PH, ramai ketua kampung dari BN. BN guna peluang tu utk tunjuk kerja dan beli jiwa penduduk setempat. peluang tu direbut BN. Yg bagi bantuan PH, yg dpt nama BN."
>
> *Analytical note: This is a critical finding — BN-aligned ketua kampung act as intermediaries who claim credit for PH government aid. The credit-attribution gap is a systemic vulnerability across FELDA and rural areas. This pattern spans NS-05, NS-11, and NS-14.*

**Confidence Rating:** **Moderate** — Separa Bandar and Luar Bandar patterns are well-corroborated, but 24 unclassified records limit locality-type analysis. Orang Asli access issue is single-source but consistent with known patterns.

**Recommended Action:**
- Deploy FELDA-specific counter-messaging: communicate that STR/SARA aid comes from PH government, not UMNO
- Establish PH presence at Orang Asli settlements currently BN-only access — coordinate with community liaison
- Prioritize lampu jalan and infrastructure quick-wins in Senaling-Inas corridor
- Address Rohingya concern in Sikamat through factual government policy communication
- Classify the 24 unclassified locality records for complete locality-type analysis

**Escalation Triggers Met:**
- ⚠️ Operational Blind Spot identified: Orang Asli settlements with BN-only access
- ⚠️ Rapidly Changing Locality: N.28 Kota showing polarized results across localities within same DUN

### NS-07: Voter Sentiment and Support Movement

**Priority Intelligence Question:**
Which issues are increasing or reducing support across Negeri Sembilan, and where is the movement concentrated?

**Assessment:**

The audit data provides the most comprehensive ground-level issue mapping available. Three dominant patterns emerge that cross multiple DUNs and represent sustained negative sentiment vectors:

**Pattern 1: STR Aid Reduction (RM100→RM50)**

This is the most significant single-issue sentiment pattern in the audit data. Multiple respondents across multiple DUNs report that SSTR cash aid was reduced from RM100 to RM50 after Anwar Ibrahim became PM. This is perceived as a broken promise or deliberate reduction.

> "laki dia dri dpat sara 100 jadi 50"
> *(N.02 Pertang — auditor: ryqall, respondent: norlaijan bt ibrahim)*

> "dapat sara 100 jadi 50" — "ex bendahari pkr, dulu dia dapat segala bantuan skrang STR dapat 50 je lain2 bantuan tak dapat"
> *(N.02 Pertang — auditor: ryqall, respondent: mad shah bin mad isa)*

> "isu sara 100" — "sbb nak tebus kne keluar jauh"
> *(N.14 Ampangan — auditor: ryqall, respondent: zulkefli b sarif)*

> "RESPONDEN MENERIMA BANTUAN (RM100 SETIAP BULAN) TETAPI SAUDARA MARA TIDAK DAPAT BULANAN, ONE-OFF RM100"
> *(N.34 Gemas — auditor: budakdato, respondent: NORZELILAH BINTI MOHAMED)*

*Analytical note: The STR reduction narrative is CORROBORATED across at least 3 DUNs (Pertang, Ampangan, Gemas) by multiple auditors. The daily brief notes Anwar announced SARA increase on 23 Jul at Paroi — this is a direct response to the STR reduction narrative. The timing suggests the campaign leadership is aware of this sentiment pattern. However, the audit data was collected 18-20 July, before the SARA announcement. Updated ground verification is needed to assess whether the SARA announcement has neutralized this narrative.*

**Pattern 2: Racial Narrative Weaponization**

Racial sentiment is being actively deployed at grassroots level across multiple DUNs. This is not incidental — it is a systematic opposition strategy.

> "dia penjawat awam lebih kpd bn sbb isu perkauman, dap kawal pmx, tpi bila terangkan dia mcm faham yg semua itu hanya dakyah untuk menakutkan org melayu"
> *(N.02 Pertang — auditor: ryqall, respondent: meor mohd zaki)*

> "Sangup undi PN jika BN tidak bertanding. Kerap bermain sentimen perkauman seperti melayu dan cina. Dia juga ada sebut mahu melayu memerintah."
> *(N.03 Sungai Lui — auditor: amirulredz19, respondent: Siti Norehan Manap)*

> "kalau boleh tak nak cina memerintah. nak melayu bersatu. tak peduli pasal isu duit naik ke? janji melayu islam memerintah"
> *(N.13 Sikamat — auditor: Norahazel91, respondent: normadiah binti rentah)*

> "Subject melihat Dap sebagai ancaman melayu Islam. Sebelum ni pengundi PH dlm 2-3 pru kebelakangan ni, tp bersedia memilih BN utk next Pru"

> "kuat bermain sentimen cina dan melayu"
> *(N.28 Kota — auditor: farahintan, respondent: Mohd Shahrin)*

*Analytical note: The racial narrative pattern is CORROBORATED across at least 5 DUNs (Pertang, Sungai Lui, Sikamat, Nilai, Kota) by 5 different auditors. The narrative has two components: (1) "DAP controls government" (DAP kawal PMX / DAP kontrol gov) and (2) "Malay unity needed to prevent Chinese rule." This is systematically deployed through ketua kampung, influencers, and social media. The first auditor quote is particularly significant — it shows the narrative CAN be countered through direct explanation ("bila terangkan dia mcm faham"), suggesting this is a persuasion opportunity, not a fixed position.*

**Pattern 3: Welfare Delivery Gap**

Welfare/kebajikan is the second-highest issue (39 mentions) and intersects with the STR reduction pattern. Multiple respondents report that aid is not reaching eligible families, or that the reduction in aid is felt as a broken promise.

### NS-09: Youth and First-Time Voter Participation

**Priority Intelligence Question:**
What political, economic and social issues are shaping participation among younger and first-time voters?

**Assessment:**

Youth political disengagement is a CORROBORATED finding across multiple DUNs. The audit reveals that young voters are not merely undecided — they are actively disengaged from the political process, creating both a turnout risk and a persuasion vacuum that opposition narratives can fill.

> "Generasi millennials dan alpha tidak celik politik dan tidak tahu apa apa. Mereka akan undi ikut keluarga atau circle mereka. Mereka senang terpengaruh dgn sosmed dan trend. Pelajar umur 18-23 lebih fokus kepada studies dan tidak ambil tahu isu tempatan dalam kawasan. Majoriti tidak berminat dengan politik."
> *(N.21 Bukit Kepayang — auditor: Chaa, respondent: Iszahraa Regina)*

> "pengundi muda cina guna chatgpt untuk sokong siapa"
> *(N.28 Kota — auditor: mikhael, public domain observation)*

> "kawasan Sikamat ni kurang aktiviti untuk golongan muda"
> *(N.13 Sikamat — auditor: Norahazel91, respondent: Nur shafikah binti abdul hamid)*

> "kefahaman politik kurang utk org muda"
> *(N.36 Repah — auditor: Ducuva, respondent: Mustapa)*

*Analytical note: The youth disengagement pattern is CORROBORATED across 4 DUNs (Bukit Kepayang, Kota, Sikamat, Repah) by 4 different auditors. Key findings: (1) Millennials and Gen Alpha are "tidak celik politik" — politically illiterate; (2) They vote based on family/circle influence, not independent assessment; (3) They are susceptible to social media trends; (4) Chinese youth are reportedly using ChatGPT to decide voting — this represents a new information-seeking behaviour that the campaign has not addressed; (5) Lack of youth-specific activities, especially for young women. The N.21 Bukit Kepayang DUN, with 2 of 3 records classified "Hard to Win," has youth apathy as its primary risk factor.*

*The ChatGPT observation is particularly significant — if young voters are using AI tools to make voting decisions, the campaign needs to ensure PH content is accurately represented in the information ecosystem these tools draw from. This is a NS-15 (misinformation) and NS-14 (narrative) intersection.*

**Confidence Rating:** **Moderate** — Pattern is corroborated across 4 DUNs but youth-specific audit questions were not systematically asked. The ChatGPT observation is single-source but represents a novel and potentially significant trend.

**Recommended Action:**
- Develop youth-specific engagement programs, particularly for young women (N.13 Sikamat)
- Investigate the ChatGPT/AI voting decision pattern — assess what information AI tools surface about PH candidates and policies
- Deploy social media content targeting 18-23 demographic with local issue relevance
- Create "show of force" youth machinery events in N.21 Bukit Kepayang where youth apathy is the primary risk
- Ensure PH content is SEO-optimized and accurately represented in sources that AI tools index

**Escalation Triggers Met:**
- ⚠️ Turnout risk identified in N.21 Bukit Kepayang — youth demographic may not turn out
- ⚠️ New information-seeking behaviour (AI/ChatGPT) not addressed in current campaign strategy

### NS-10: Candidate Suitability and Acceptance

**Priority Intelligence Question:**
Which candidates strengthen or weaken electoral competitiveness within each DUN?

**Assessment:**

The audit data reveals candidate acceptance issues in at least 2 DUNs, both involving PKR candidates. The pattern is consistent: local PKR machinery is not fully supporting the candidate due to perceived conflicts of interest or candidate-cabang relationship breakdowns.

**Case 1: N.03 Sungai Lui — PKR Machinery Not Supporting Candidate**

> "Beliau juga merupakan Ketua kordinasi calon, dan orang bertangungjawab susun jadual calon, dan kajian data pengundi di DUN Sungai Lui Nampak beliau sedikit kecewa dengan jentera parti di sana kerana beliau kata jentera tidak support calon sangat sebab ada isu calon terima dana dari BN. namun Penerimaan calon masih dapat di terima memandangkan calon merupakan bekas cikgu sekolah di sini."
> *(N.03 Sungai Lui — auditor: amirulredz19, respondent: Zamzul, PKR candidate coordination lead)*

*Analytical note: The candidate (former schoolteacher) has local acceptance due to professional history, but PKR machinery is withholding support due to allegation that candidate received funding from BN. ## CROSS-PIR INTELLIGENCE THEMES

The audit data reveals patterns that span multiple PIRs and represent the most significant strategic findings:

### Theme 1: The Credit Attribution Gap (NS-05 × NS-07 × NS-11 × NS-14)

**Pattern:** PH government delivers welfare aid (STR, SARA, Budi95), but BN-aligned ketua kampung and UMNO machinery claim credit at grassroots level. Voters believe aid comes from UMNO, not the PH government. This creates a paradox: PH is delivering but not benefiting electorally.

**Evidence chain:**
- NS-05: "Yg bagi bantuan PH, yg dpt nama BN" (N.34 Gemas)
- NS-07: STR reduction narrative — voters blame PH for reducing aid they don't realize PH provides
- NS-11: "org felda lebih berat utk mengundi UMNO sbb igt bantuan semua dtg dari UMNO" (N.36 Repah)
- NS-14: "DAP kontrol gov" — when aid is delivered, it reinforces the DAP-control narrative

**Strategic implication:** This is the single most important finding from the audit. The credit attribution gap means PH is governing but not campaigning — the government's achievements are invisible at the point of delivery because intermediaries are BN-aligned. This requires a structural counter-strategy, not just campaign-period messaging.

### Theme 2: The Racial Narrative Ecosystem (NS-07 × NS-11 × NS-14)

**Pattern:** The "DAP kontrol gov" → "sentimen perkauman" → "boneka DAP" narrative chain is a self-reinforcing ecosystem deployed systematically through ketua kampung and social media. It operates even in Malay-majority constituencies with Malay PH representatives.

**Evidence chain:**
- NS-07: Racial sentiment corroborated across 5+ DUNs
- NS-11: "sentimen kaum di mainkan di peringkat akar umbi. ketua kampung main peranan besar (BN)" (N.07)
- NS-14: "DAP kontrol gov" corroborated across 4+ DUNs; "boneka DAP" active beyond Sikamat

**Strategic implication:** The racial narrative cannot be countered by a single speech or at a single DUN. It requires a statewide, sustained, Malay-language counter-messaging strategy deployed through alternative community channels that bypass BN-controlled ketua kampung.

### Theme 3: PKR Machinery Friction (NS-10 × NS-16)

**Pattern:** PKR machinery is not supporting candidates in at least 2 DUNs due to candidate-cabang conflicts, alleged BN funding, and factional disputes. This compromises GOTV operations in competitive seats.

**Evidence chain:**
- NS-10: "jentera tidak support calon sangat sebab ada isu calon terima dana dari BN" (N.03 Sungai Lui)
- NS-10: "calon ada masaalah dgn ketua cabang, masa pengumuman nama calon kena boo dgn ahli" (N.14 Ampangan)
- NS-16: BGU funding constraints compound machinery friction
- NS-16: AMK absent in Tampin area; PKR members absent in Gemencheh

**Strategic implication:** PKR needs internal conflict resolution before polling day. The pattern suggests candidate selection processes may not have adequate cabang-level consultation, leading to rejection by local machinery.

### Theme 4: The Funding-Morale-Visibility Nexus (NS-05 × NS-16)

**Pattern:** BGU funding gaps lead to low machinery visibility (no flags, billboards), which leads to morale problems (comparisons with BN machinery), which leads to reduced ground operations, which leads to voter perception that PH is not serious about contesting.

**Evidence chain:**
- NS-16: "Kekurangan kemeriahan dapat dilihat dimana jentera tidak bergerak laju kerana tiada dana" (N.09 Lenggeng)
- NS-16: "BGU perlukan dana untuk bergerak" (N.34 Gemas)
- NS-16: "ade yg bertanya pasal token sbb akan buat perbandingan dengan jentera BN" (N.36 Repah)
- NS-05: "Pengundi kawasan boleh swing kepada PH sekiranya jentera menunjukkan show of force" (N.09 Lenggeng)

**Strategic implication:** Emergency campaign funding for visible materials (flags, billboards) is needed in Lenggeng, Gemas, and Repah. The "show of force" is identified by auditors as a direct swing factor — voters are more likely to support PH if they see visible campaign presence.

### Theme 5: Youth Information Vacuum (NS-09 × NS-14 × NS-15)

**Pattern:** Young voters are politically disengaged and susceptible to social media narratives. A new trend — using ChatGPT to make voting decisions — means AI-surfaced information about candidates and parties may determine youth votes.

**Evidence chain:**
- NS-09: "Generasi millennials dan alpha tidak celik politik" (N.21 Bukit Kepayang)
- NS-09: "pengundi muda cina guna chatgpt untuk sokong siapa" (N.28 Kota)
- NS-14: Racial narratives spread through social media and influencers
- NS-15: Misinformation risk — if AI tools surface opposition narratives, they become "verified" for young voters

**Strategic implication:** The campaign needs to ensure PH content is accurately and prominently represented in the information ecosystem that AI tools (ChatGPT, Gemini, etc.) draw from. This is a novel challenge that traditional campaign strategies do not address.

## COLLECTION REQUIREMENTS — Post-Audit Intelligence Gaps

### Gap 1: Uncovered T1 Battleground Seats (PRIORITY — CRITICAL)

**Gap:** 19 of 36 DUNs have no detailed audit records. Critical T1 seats without audit data include:
- N.25 Paroi (3-cornered contest; Anwar ceramah; SARA announcement venue)
- N.32 Linggi (Aminuddin's new seat; BN manifesto launch venue 24 Jul; cumulative -35.7% sentiment decline)
- N.20 Labu (3-cornered; PH active ground campaign; "sekolah kurang" issue)
- N.04 Klawang (3-cornered with cousins; Bakri Sawir vs Danni Rais vs Adib Musa)
- N.23 Senawang (SARA alignment area; Jelajah Kekal Harapan)

**Required collection:** Deploy auditors to these 5 T1 seats immediately. Priority: N.32 Linggi (BN manifesto launch tomorrow) and N.25 Paroi (SARA announcement venue).

### Gap 2: SARA Announcement Ground Verification (PRIORITY — HIGH)

**Gap:** The audit was conducted 18-20 July, before Anwar's SARA increase announcement on 23 July. The STR reduction narrative was the most significant sentiment pattern in the audit. We do not know whether the SARA announcement has neutralized this narrative at ground level.

**Required collection:** Re-audit respondents in N.02 Pertang, N.14 Ampangan, and N.34 Gemas (where STR reduction was most acutely reported) to assess whether SARA announcement has changed sentiment. Deploy within 24 hours.

### Gap 3: Orang Asli Settlement Access (PRIORITY — HIGH)

**Gap:** BN-only access to Orang Asli settlements (Kg Orang Asli Air Sungai Jernih, N.35 Gemencheh) represents a structural intelligence blind spot. We have no information on voter sentiment, PH presence, or access opportunities in these settlements.

**Required collection:** Map all Orang Asli settlements across NS DUNs. Identify access barriers. Assess whether PH can establish community liaison. Coordinate with Orang Asli community organizations.

### Gap 4: BN FELDA Ketua Kampung Network (PRIORITY — HIGH)

**Gap:** The audit reveals BN-aligned ketua kampung claiming credit for PH aid, but we lack a comprehensive map of BN-aligned vs PH-aligned ketua kampung across FELDA areas.

**Required collection:** Map all ketua kampung in FELDA-settlement DUNs (Pertang, Sungai Lui, Palong, Jeram Padang, Gemas, Gemencheh, Repah). Classify alignment. Identify alternative community liaison channels for BN-aligned areas.

### Gap 5: ChatGPT/AI Voting Pattern (PRIORITY — MODERATE)

**Gap:** The observation that Chinese youth use ChatGPT to decide voting is single-source and not investigated. We do not know: (1) How widespread this is, (2) What information ChatGPT surfaces about PH candidates and policies, (3) Whether opposition narratives are over-represented in AI-surfaced content.

**Required collection:** Test ChatGPT and other AI tools with NS-specific voting queries. Assess content accuracy and narrative balance. Determine whether PH content is adequately represented in indexed sources.

### Gap 6: Postal/Diaspora Voter Sentiment (PRIORITY — MODERATE)

**Gap:** One audit record (N.27 Rantau) captures diaspora voter sentiment — M40 frustration with tax burden vs B40 benefits. We lack systematic data on postal voter sentiment across NS.

**Required collection:** Deploy targeted outreach to diaspora/postal voter communities. Assess whether M40 tax frustration is widespread or isolated. Connect to PH manifesto tax relief provisions.

### Gap 7: Component Party Mobilisation in Tampin Area (PRIORITY — MODERATE)

**Gap:** In N.35 Gemencheh, only Amanah members found — PKR and DAP absent. In N.36 Repah, AMK absent. We lack data on component party mobilisation across the Tampin parliamentary seat (P.133).

**Required collection:** Audit component party presence across all P.133 Tampin DUNs (N.34 Gemas, N.35 Gemencheh, N.36 Repah). Assess whether PKR/DAP mobilisation failure is systemic or localized.

### Gap 8: BN Manifesto Response (PRIORITY — HIGH)

**Gap:** BN manifesto launch is scheduled for 24 July at DUN Linggi + Pertang. The 50k jobs promise (vs PH's 30k) is a "game changer" claim. We have no ground-level data on how voters are receiving BN's manifesto messaging.

**Required collection:** Deploy auditors to N.32 Linggi and N.02 Pertang for BN manifesto launch day (24 Jul). Capture crowd response, voter reactions, BN-PN joint framing. Priority for next audit cycle.
